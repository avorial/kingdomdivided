# History

While their prehistory is chronicled in the [[Legend-of-the-Maw|Legend of the Maw]]. The people as a whole naturally settled in the North and the East, there are runic cairn's sprinkled all over the land in the time before the mass conversion of the the Fedor people by Ethen Sedi during the first Bellringer crusade. Many people still talk of their ancestor who was with Angus, such as the Henge's say that when the left the maw that they had never seen a horse and the first to ride one was the founder of [[House-Henge|House Henge]].

# Stereotypes

Fedor are commonly seen as violent and unruly by other people of the Belllands. Mostly this is because fedor are stand offish to other ancestries until they have proven themselves.

# Biological traits

Many of the Fedor are born with the red hair of Angus and the Chosen, but not all it's about 70%. People with darker hair are considered colloquially to be considered sneaky and devious in more rural areas. Also visons are more common among the Fedor, and it's seen as a blessing.

# Language

While every Fedor is raised to speak the common tongue of the bellringers. Some slang uses the old words from the land of fire and flowers normally only Old women and scholars have taken the time to learn the runic language of Angus and his people to it's fullest extend and are able to write in it. For ease of discussion it's called “Fedor Runes”, even though there is a strong spoken context.

# Mechanics & Rules

## Blood of the Fedors Quality

Increase your Combat Defense by +2. When testing Warfare (Tactics), you may re-roll a number of 1s equal to your Cunning rank.

# Inspiration Concept

Fedor are based on Celtic and Viking people of the 1000's, while the nobles are given a feel of early American's.
