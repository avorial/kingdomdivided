# History

House Rolland: Guardians of Northton

House Rolland, a loyal banner house to House Whitestar, holds its seat of power in the coastal city of Northton, situated at the mouth of the northern river. For generations, the Rollands have stood as stalwart protectors of their domain, facing the challenges of piracy and safeguarding the interests of House Whitestar. However, their history took a tragic turn almost two decades ago when the untimely demise of Killian and Asling Rolland shook the foundations of the house.

Killian and Asling, beloved leaders of House Rolland, met their demise in a tragic event known as the Dark Tides. It was a stormy night when a fierce tempest struck the shores of Northton, ravaging the city and causing devastating floods. In the chaos and destruction, the lives of the esteemed couple were claimed, leaving the house and the city in mourning.

In the aftermath of the Dark Tides, Northton became vulnerable to pirate raids, as the absence of strong leadership created a power vacuum. Pirate factions, enticed by the city's strategic coastal location and weakened defenses, descended upon Northton like vultures, seeking to exploit the chaos and seize control. House Rolland, under the leadership of the eldest sibling, Muireann Rolland, valiantly fought to repel the ongoing assaults, but their resources were stretched thin, and the pirates proved persistent.

Northton, once a bustling trade hub, suffered greatly from the constant pirate raids. The economy faltered, and the city's prosperity waned as fear and uncertainty permeated the streets. The commoners faced the brunt of the hardships, their livelihoods disrupted and their safety compromised. The Rollands, burdened with the responsibility of protecting their people, had to navigate the delicate balance between defending their domain and addressing the needs of their citizens.

The pirate threat was not homogeneous. Specific pirate factions, each with their own distinct methods and motivations, posed a significant danger to Northton. The notorious Black Scorpion Brotherhood, infamous for their ruthless tactics and cunning maneuvers, struck fear into the hearts of sailors and citizens alike. The relentless Sea Wolves, a nomadic pirate horde, prowled the coastal waters, showing no mercy to their victims. House Rolland faced the daunting task of combating these entrenched pirate factions while grappling with limited resources and the safety of their people.

The relationship between House Rolland and House Whitestar is one built on mutual respect and cooperation. Both houses share a deep allegiance to the kingdom and a commitment to maintaining stability and order. However, the geographical distance between Northton and the heart of House Whitestar's power has occasionally given rise to tensions and uncertainties. As House Rolland faces the constant challenges of pirate raids and struggles to protect their coastal city, they may question the extent of their connection to House Whitestar and contemplate forging new alliances to strengthen their position.

Within the city of Northton, influential figures emerge who may challenge House Rolland's authority or seek to undermine their rule. Captain [[alaric-ironhook-wycliff|Alaric "Ironhook" Wycliff]], a former pirate who has gained a degree of power within Northton, remains an enigmatic presence. His transition from a ruthless raider to a cunning businessman has raised suspicions about his motives and alliances. Captain Wycliff, using his influence to sway public opinion, challenges House Rolland's leadership and adds an additional layer of complexity to Northton's political landscape.

The Fedor heritage of House Rolland shapes their identity and interactions with other houses and factions in the region. Descendants of the fierce Fedor people, known for their Viking-like demeanor, the Rollands have successfully cultivated a civilized facade while retaining elements of their combative past. This unique blend of refined nobility and underlying martial prowess sets them apart from their counterparts and influences how they navigate the complex web of alliances and conflicts in the region.

While House Rolland may not possess ancient legends or artifacts directly associated with their lineage, they do carry a fading tradition of magical tattoos. These mystical markings, once highly regarded, have fallen out of favor in recent years, viewed as relics of the past and overshadowed by the rise of Bellringer faith. However, the remnants of this ancient art form remain within the family, hinting at the potential for rediscovery and the resurgence of forgotten powers.

As House Rolland grapples with the ongoing pirate threat, internal power struggles, and the delicate balance between their Fedor heritage and their evolving domesticity, the story of their struggle to protect Northton and redefine their role in the Kingdom Divided unfolds. Their resilience, resourcefulness, and ability to forge alliances will be tested as they strive to restore peace and prosperity to their beloved city and uphold the honor of House Rolland.

It is said that this mystical island holds the key to unlocking the true potential of House Rolland and restoring the glory of Northton. The exact location and nature of Tír na Mara remain shrouded in mystery, waiting to be discovered by those bold enough to venture into uncharted waters.

It is believed that the tragic deaths of Killian and Asling Rolland were not mere happenstance but rather a result of the curse inflicted upon them by a rival house seeking to weaken House Rolland's influence. The identity of the house and the means to break the curse remain concealed, yet the desire for justice and retribution stirs within the hearts of House Rolland's loyal retainers.
