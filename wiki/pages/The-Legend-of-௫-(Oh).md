# Starts With An End

While all things want, almost nothing wants all. There was a time, and a time to be where the want of all and the ability to take it would be imbued in one form. This form would grow, from a common man to a Noble, to a King, to an Emperor, to a God, to the devourer of Gods. This Devourer will take all that is the land, all that is the sea, all that is the sky and it will all be eaten by him. This has happened and will happen again. But all is not truly immortal, all things will die, some lasting as a firefly, and others as long-lived as a star, thus even the devourer will die. The devourer in this epoch was named ௫, Scholars for simplicity have just called it "O". As O finished the great meal, he realized he himself was dying, not quickly by any means but still dying.

As all thinking creatures do when they feel death's grasp, he screamed in the darkness just to at least hear something, but nothing would come to his ears because he had devoured all sound. In this sadness, he began to cry, and these tears made the Star Gods the first living companions for O.  The stars knew that O was dying, and tried to comfort him, but some of the stars were scared, they kept their distance for fear O's hunger would rise again.

Death comes in many ways, and for O it was an illness. As the illness grew and O coughed expelling some of the worm-gods that were killing him from the inside. The stars were fearful if O saw the Worm Gods, he might start to eat again, so they kept their light off of them. Over time the Worm Gods broke O into 7 pieces. These are the largest things in the sky.

The Sun was made from his head still devouring all, and our planet was made with his heart. All that was before came out of O as he decayed. Thus was reborn the Old gods of land and sea. While they had power, their memories were shattered having gone through the digestion of O.

## Land of the Heart

As the Heart cooled from the heat of O, it became the sphere we know as the "Heartstone". The blood drained into the lower valves, and the blood turned clear but still was salty as birthed our seas. As the gods awoke, the trees and the rocks lived, and the storms sang. They thought they were masters of this sphere, and they made great kingdoms of men and monsters. The greatest of these empires was called Kadal they built cities on all the lands, thinking themselves masters of Heartstone. But they were not alone, the worm-gods were still eating the heart. O was not fully dead, and the heart started to beat. The world shook and the blood seas boiled. The Gods of the Heart, saw their creations destroyed and called for aid. The stars were silent. But the Worm Gods heard. A pact was made by the powers of the Gods of the Heart and the Worm Gods. Together they found the spark of O in the heartstone and destroyed it. As the Heartstone calmed, the treaty was forgotten.

### The Lands

The landmasses were broken by the beating, and the largest became what is now known as:

- Kadal - The Undying lands
- Mikado Empire
- The Holy Lands of Fygor
- The Leandan League
- The Bell Lands (The Land of Earth and Water)

## The Lands of Earth and Water

In the lands of the Esten Kings, old gods took form and became an Empire.

## The Worm's Teeth

In the Mikado, the knowledge of the worm was taught to man, showing him the connections and the ways to break them in reality. They were all marked though by connecting to the heartstone, by becoming one with the creatures that lived on it.

## Leandan Independence

In the Leandan ethos, deeply rooted in the teachings of Kato Leandan, lies the belief that the next incarnation of O will erupt from their nation, spilling all and one into the sea. Embracing this prophecy, Kato Leandan imparted the qualities of O as the guiding principles for building their society. As Leandans strive to create a society of abundance and organization, they recognize that only from such fertile grounds can the next incarnation of O emerge. To maintain their individuality and honor their beliefs, Leandans practice cremation, viewing it as a symbolic return to the abundant earth. Whispers persist of secret burials among some, like the Zafroff, who cling to their Kadalian traditions in the face of Leadan evolution.

## The Time of the Stars Return

The shy stars seeing the death of O, returned and saw the men that had been born from fire and rain. Men are ignored by the gods of worm and heart alike. They spoke to them of the future for they remembered the worlds of the past, they spoke to men of the Fygor as they grew into great nations.
