# Empires of Cestona

## Kadal the Undying Lands

### Origins and Ascendancy

The Empire of Kadal traces its origins to the dawn of civilization on Cestona, a sphere resonating with arcane energies. The Avestan, the first race to awaken after the calming of the spheres, established a strict and hierarchical society where every individual was either a master or a slave, often both, creating an intricate web of power and subjugation. This structure allowed energies to flow upwards, like a dark pyramid scheme, with the Masked Senate at its apex. The Masked Senate, immortal and faceless behind their ancient masks, wielded absolute power, unseen and unchallenged.

### The Masked Senate and the Hierarchical Bond

The Masked Senate ruled from the capital, a grand city surrounded by ancient walls and filled with arcane wonders. The Senate's masks, symbols of their eternal rule, ensured continuity even in death, as successors would don the masks, perpetuating the illusion of immortality. Under their governance, the Kadalans built a formidable empire, drawing power through the bonds of slavery that linked every citizen in a chain of subservience and dominance. This magical hierarchy allowed the Senate to channel immense energies from the lower echelons, maintaining their power and extending their reign.

### The Heartbeat and the Worm Gods

The empire's zenith was shattered by a catastrophic event known as the Heartbeat. This event, a cosmic disturbance, awakened the Worm gods—ancient entities that gnawed at the edges of Cestona. The Heartbeat's pulses disrupted the delicate balance of magic, causing chaos and weakening the empire. Desperate to halt the devastation, the Senate dispatched a contingent of their finest mages and warriors to confront the Worm gods. They never returned, leaving the empire in a state of perpetual uncertainty.

### Isolation and Decline

In the wake of the Heartbeat, the Kadalans retreated into their capital, fortifying their city and sealing their borders. The once-thriving empire adopted a policy of isolationism, fearing the outside world and the potential threats it harbored. The shores of Kadal, once bustling with trade and culture, became heavily guarded, allowing only a select few to leave. These outcasts ventured into the lands now occupied by new and ascendant societies, bearing the legacy and remnants of their forgotten empire.

Within the walls of the capital, the Masked Senate clung to power, their magic waning but still potent enough to maintain control. They lived in constant fear and denial, convinced that the cessation of the Heartbeat signaled a triumph rather than a reprieve. As the centuries passed, they struggled to piece together the fragments of their illustrious past, their memories fragmented and their knowledge incomplete.

### Encounters with New Civilizations

The outcasts who ventured beyond the guarded shores found a world transformed. New civilizations had risen from the ashes of the Kadalans' fall, each with its unique culture and magical practices. These societies, built upon the remnants of the ancient empire, viewed the Kadalans with a mix of curiosity and suspicion. The outcasts, carrying the weight of their heritage, became both emissaries and pariahs, bridging the gap between the old world and the new.

### Legacy and the Path Forward

In the shadows of their capital, the Masked Senate grappled with the echoes of their past. They yearned for the lost glory of their empire, unaware that the true source of their downfall lay in their ignorance of the lingering threats. The Worm gods, though dormant, still posed a danger, their presence a constant reminder of the empire's vulnerability. As the world outside their borders evolved, the Senate remained shrouded in mystery, guarding not only their city but also the secrets that could either save or doom them once again.

The Empire of Kadal, once a beacon of power and magic, now stands as a testament to the perils of hubris and isolation. The Kadalans, with their intricate hierarchy and arcane mastery, remain a shadow of their former selves, clinging to the hope that their forgotten legends might one day be restored.

## Fygor The Holy Kingdom

In the aftermath of the Oh's death , the fall of the comets marked not only the awakening of mankind to the visions of the future but also the rise of a Theocratic Bureaucracy that gripped the nascent civilization in its pious embrace. The people, in their newfound prosperity, felt a deep indebtedness to the Star Gods who had guided them through the tapestry of time, revealing the secrets of both past and future. The Council of Cardinals emerged as the earthly stewards of this divine wisdom, each member serving as the conduit between the mortal realm and the celestial deities.

As the Council tightened its grip on the affairs of the land, it inadvertently created fault lines within the society it sought to govern. The once-unified vision of the future began to fracture, and the seeds of dissent took root among the nobility. The fractures deepened until they reached a breaking point, and from this schism emerged the enigmatic Bellringer, a Stargod who sensed the vulnerabilities of the system and seized upon them.

The Bellringer rallied the disenchanted nobility, offering promises of greater autonomy and freedom from the suffocating grasp of the Council. What ensued was a civil war that shook the foundations of the theocratic regime. The conflict pitted the traditional pantheon of Star Gods, embodied by the Council of Cardinals, against the insurgent forces led by the Bellringer and their noble allies.

Though destined for defeat from the outset, the rebellion served a purpose that transcended the immediate struggle for power. The Council emerged victorious, banishing the Bellringers and their followers from the realm they had sought to liberate. Exiled and scattered, these rebels embarked on a journey to find their own way in the world, vowing to one day return and challenge the established order.

The aftermath of the conflict left scars on the once-perfect tapestry of prophecy woven by the Council. The tight control they once exerted over the future began to erode as whispers of dissent and newfound independence echoed among the nobility. The nobles, having tasted the power of rebellion, now sought greater autonomy, challenging the once-unquestioned authority of the Council of Cardinals.

As the ages changed, the people clamored for a different kind of governance—one that balanced divine guidance with individual freedom. The seeds of revolution were sown, and the stage was set for a new era where the destinies of the people would be shaped not only by the celestial gods but also by the choices of mortals determined to forge their own path in the ever-evolving tapestry of fate.

## Mikado Empire

In the heart of the Mikado Empire, a realm known for its small size yet immense power, a unique connection between its people and the enigmatic Worm Gods flourished. These divine entities, residing in the depths of the earth, bestowed upon the inhabitants the ability to merge with the essence of beasts. This mystical union between man and creature enabled the denizens of the Mikado Empire to harness extraordinary powers, transforming their society into a bastion of prosperity and self-sufficiency.

The priests, acting as intermediaries between the people and the Worm Gods, became the architects of the land's transformation. Through the art of terraforming, they cultivated vast expanses of fertile terrain, ensuring bountiful harvests that sustained the empire's populace. Moreover, these skilled priests could transmute ordinary rocks into exquisite marble and gemstones, adorning the Mikado Empire with unparalleled beauty and wealth. The empire stood as a testament to the harmonious blend of nature, magic, and human ingenuity.

However, this golden age faced an unforeseen challenge when followers of the Star Gods, bearers of a rival magic, arrived at the borders of the Mikado Empire. As the influence of the Star Gods encroached, the once unassailable magic of the Worm Gods began to wane. This erosion struck at the very foundation of Mikado society, threatening its prosperity and stability.

In response to this existential threat, the Mikado Empire called upon its highest nobles, initiating a fierce competition among them. Each noble house vied for the prestigious title of Ai Hoku, the Star Eater—a symbolic protector against the encroaching influence of the Star Gods. The quest for this title was not merely a struggle for power but a solemn duty to safeguard the empire's unique connection with the Worm Gods.

The competition among the noble houses became a grand spectacle, with magical duels, intricate displays of terraforming prowess, and diplomatic intrigue. Success in this endeavor not only brought glory and honor to the winning house but solidified their position as first among equals, guiding the destiny of the Mikado Empire.

As the ages passed, the title of Ai Hoku shifted from one noble house to another, marking the ebb and flow of magical prowess and societal influence. The changing of the guard symbolized the adaptability of the Mikado people in the face of adversity, ensuring that their connection with the Worm Gods remained strong, even in the ever-shifting tides of cosmic magic. Thus, the Mikado Empire endured, a testament to the resilience of a people shaped by the profound interplay between man, beast, and the divine.

## The Ledain League

The isolated southern land mass, cut off from the rest of the world for seven centuries after the Heartbeat, underwent a profound transformation. In the absence of external influences, the society that emerged was marked by a unique ethos that valued individuals based on their material wealth and influence rather than religious affiliations. This departure from traditional norms led to the establishment of a complex guild structure, with each guild vying for supremacy in a world shaped by power and prosperity.

The intense competition between guilds eventually escalated into devastating conflicts, resulting in widespread loss of life and chaos. Recognizing the urgent need for stability and unity, the leaders of the most influential guilds came together to form the League of Peace. This league served as a unifying force, allowing each major council to purchase a seat, the strength of which was directly proportional to the financial contribution made. The funds collected were utilized to create a formidable standing army, tasked with maintaining order, defending against external threats, and quelling internal conflicts among guilds that threatened the newfound peace.

Despite being technologically advanced, the southern land faced unique challenges. The relatively low population posed a significant limitation, hindering the expansion of their influence beyond their borders. Additionally, the society lacked any connection to magic, a force wielded by many neighboring nations, which further complicated their diplomatic and military endeavors. The absence of magical prowess and the inability to forge a unified front with the powerful guilds made it difficult for the southern land to assert dominance over other empires.

In the face of external pressures and internal strife, the League of Peace found itself at the center of a delicate balancing act. While their advanced technology gave them a military edge, their diplomatic and strategic maneuvering required finesse. The league became a symbol of hope for those who sought stability in a world torn by conflicts, yet the challenges ahead were daunting. As the southern land grappled with its unique identity and sought to navigate the complexities of power, wealth, and the absence of magic, the fate of this isolated society hung in the balance.
