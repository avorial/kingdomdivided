## [¶](#the-burning-divide-the-emperor-and-the-ai-hoku) **The Burning Divide: The Emperor and the Ai Hoku**

---

### [¶](#the-crowned-flame-mikado-xelin-iv) **The Crowned Flame: Mikado Xelin IV**

Emperor Xelin IV was crowned in 222 under a handclasp eclipse that bathed the capital in crimson. From the beginning, his reign has been marked by silence and symbolism. Though still young, he has become more myth than man. Rarely does he speak directly. Instead, his edicts are issued through the Priests of House Maj, who interpret his utterances—poetic riddles whispered in ancient tongues that sound more like prophecy than policy.

It is widely believed that Xelin IV no longer touches the ground. Eyewitnesses claim he hovers above the jade-tiled palace floors, and a shimmering red raven has been seen pacing the upper terraces under certain lunar alignments. The priests insist this is a sign that the Worm Gods are reshaping him—that the Emperor is ascending to a new form. Others fear it means he is no longer mortal.

His detachment from earthly affairs has only grown. While he maintains absolute authority in theory, in practice the military and civil administration have become increasingly reliant on the will of the Ai Hoku.

---

### [¶](#the-iron-thorn-ai-hoku-thran-abbyean) **The Iron Thorn: Ai Hoku Thran Abbyean**

In contrast, Thran Abbyean, the Ai Hoku, is a man of momentum. Charismatic, pragmatic, and politically volatile, he rose through the ranks of the Abbyean war dynasty—once thought to be in decline. It was the campaign led by his niece, Lady Suria Abbyean, that turned the tides. Her destruction of the Bellringer navy in 220 gave the Empire uncontested control of the seas.

Since then, Thran’s successes have given him both popularity and leverage. As Master of Siege and inventor of the smoke-dragon engines, he embodies the martial and technological might of the Empire’s modern military. He tours the provinces personally, receives emissaries before the Emperor does, and has begun issuing military orders without direct ratification from the Celestial Court—like an emperor in all but name.

---

### [¶](#the-first-slight-the-crown-without-kneeling) **The First Slight: The Crown Without Kneeling**

When Xelin IV was crowned, the tradition for centuries dictated that the Ai Hoku kneel and a ritual exchange take place. The Ai Hoku would place the ancestral flame-band upon the Emperor’s brow, and the Emperor would offer a part of his form—a braid, a feather, or a scale—as a token, acknowledging the Ai Hoku as his chosen right hand.

Thran Abbyean, newly appointed Ai Hoku at the time, refused to kneel. Instead, he bowed shallowly, stating, *“The fire already burns. I only stoke it.”*

There were gasps in the crowd. But Xelin did not alter the ritual. He pulled a feather from his own plumage and placed it behind Thran’s ear. Thran nodded, turned from the Emperor, and mounted his horse. As the wind stirred, the feather fell—unnoticed by the Ai Hoku.

This moment, though seemingly minor, was a political earthquake. Within days, palace rumors spread: Was the Ai Hoku declaring himself kingmaker? Was he rejecting the divinity of the Mikado line? The Emperor said nothing.

But that silence echoed louder than any decree.

---

### [¶](#the-empire-divided-in-allegiance) **The Empire Divided in Allegiance**

Now, in 231, cracks are showing. The Empire may still be unified on maps, but in the halls of power, two visions have emerged.

**The Loyalists** believe in the eternal flame of the Emperor. To them, Xelin IV is not merely Emperor—he is the voice of the Worm Gods made flesh. His riddles are sacred truth; his transformation, holy evolution. They argue that Thran Abbyean is a mortal general drunk on battlefield glory, his ambition a creeping rot that must be checked.

**The Featherfree**, as they call themselves, rally behind the Ai Hoku. They see a paralyzed Empire ruled by a distant, unreachable Emperor, and a decisive military commander who actually protects the people. They whisper that the Empire would not falter if the throne bore a different name—and that perhaps it should.

**The Outliers**—chiefly among the cults of the Worm Gods—speak of a third path. They believe the Emperor and the Ai Hoku are each aspects of a great cycle. One voice rises. One blade strikes. They await the Convergence—when Raven and Flame will either merge… or bring ruin.
