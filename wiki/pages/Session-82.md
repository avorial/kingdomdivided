# Synopsis

The party decides to travel from Shelby to Graybell and Crystal Wood, regions characterized by mountainous terrain and terraced farming. Lyla, one of the characters, interacts with a nervous peasant who is a Bellringer, concerned about the noble presence. They arrive at a village centered around a small chapel, where the locals call their children indoors, wary but not hostile.

They are welcomed into the home of Stefanos, who offers his hospitality. During their stay, a child named Stella recounts a troubling tale of people being taken away by men who did not speak a foreign language. A tense discussion arises about various fundamentalist groups and the presence of the Mikkado. Sovereign diffuses a heated moment between Casimir and Peter.

The following day, the village is busier. They learn the banner of [[House-Liris|House Lyris]] indicates loyalty to Ethan Sedai, believed to be possessed by an old god. Casimir and Sovereign explore a pit, discovering man-made tunnels and a lake of glowing blue goo, which is identified as an entrance to the Maw.

A merchant's wares, revealed to be grave goods, provoke Lyla to confront and slap the merchant. Caden secretly buys an Esten sword. The city they are in is reminiscent of Pompeii, with Esten king sites and Bellringer priests. They meet Sir Andreas Taslan, one of the knights governing the area, who explains the history of Graybell and its governance.

The knights were chosen for their support and restraint, respectively. Andreas informs them about the imperial prince's situation and introduces them to his Esten familiar of a large Vulture it seemed to have some alien intelligence. They are assured their conversations will remain private within his home. The other knight, Sir Zisis Vathi, is depicted as fiery and forward.

Dinner with Sir Andreas Taslan and his entourage introduces them to Tiklyack, a Maw refugee enamored with the surface world. Taslan's daughters serve as handmaidens to Mercy, linking them to a spy network. A witty exchange with Sofia Taslan reveals their knowledge and connections. The conversation touches on the rare and powerful House of Fire, known for their passionate concentration of efforts.

Casimer in his interactions with the House of Fire, is seduced but leaves before anything untoward happens.
