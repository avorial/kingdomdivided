## Ancestry

- Blood of the Enisen - There is a strong debate, if the Enisen are the oldest race, or the youngest (having come from some other world.) The pure blood of their kind, on top of having bone white skin have the keenist sight in all the bell lands. Enisen keeps are kept entirely in the dark, if there are no vistors as even their children can read by the moonlight. They keep also to some superstitions that have been abonded by other races.  You may also see in starlight or moonlight as if it was day, and on a cloudy night as if it was lit by the moon and stars, candlelight is as if a torch, and a torch as if it was midday. You may also perform Survival tasks in dark environments at a +0 modifier, as long as the task is possible at all. In the case of Orientation, a mystical sense guides you in the absence of other cues. You increase your Sorcery Defense by 2.
- Blood of the Ferdors - An ancient legend says that the first of the Ferdors, was a Qintill who loved combat so much that his internal fire turned his hair red. While Ferdors don't believe that their combat prowess and tactical knowledge, comes from this ancient legend they have had many great generals come from this bloodline. Increase your Combat Defense by +2. When testing Warfare (Tactics), you may re-roll a number of 1s equal to your Cunning rank. If in a chronicle that is not commonly using Warfare you can Reroll Will (Courage)  you may re-roll a number of 1s equal to your Courage rank.
- Blood of the Paltris - In game mechanics and rules, the Paltris exemplify their hardiness and resilience. When attacked by environmental hazards, they add their Athletics rank to passive Endurance, showcasing their ability to endure and withstand the challenges presented by their surroundings. Furthermore, in tests of resilience or when foraging and hunting in their natural habitats, Paltris characters can re-roll a number of 1s equal to their Will rank, embodying their ability to adapt and persevere in the face of adversity. The Paltris possess a unique cultural aspect related to their status and engagement with others. Characters with higher Status can never automatically compel Paltris individuals, requiring them to engage in an intrigue to exert influence. This represents the Paltris' independent nature and their preference for personal agency and self-determination. They value direct engagement and open discourse, rather than being swayed by authority or social standing alone.
- Blood of the Qintills - The "Blood of the Quintill" quality signifies the inherent charisma and leadership qualities that flow through the veins of the Quintill. Characters possessing this quality gain a +2 bonus to Intimidate and Persuasion tests, representing their innate ability to sway others through charisma and authority. Additionally, when engaging in intrigues or political maneuverings, the Quintill's Status is considered one rank higher, allowing them to influence targets without necessarily entering into full-fledged intrigues.
- Blood of the Saltbloods - It is said that the Saltbloods had a name for their people, but it was lost as the communities spread across the world, Always attracted to the sea, trading and raiding became a way of life. Some of the Saltblood settled in lands, but every saltblood as spent some time on the sea. What few outsiders know is in on windless seas in dark nights ancient mysteries have been told sailor to sailor. Few focus their lives on these mysteries, but those that do are feared by all. Add +2 to the results of tests you make to Bargain or Intimidate during an intrigue. Furthermore, you have been initiated into one sorcerous Tradition favored by your ancestors, and have been initiated into one of its Arts, though you gain no Works. You may study and learn sorcerous Works of that Art normally. See Chapter 6: Sorcery for details.
- Blood of the Zafroff - The "Blood of the Zafroff" quality represents the inherent drive for perfection and insatiable hunger for mastery that pulses through the veins of the Zafroff. Characters possessing this quality gain the ability to learn abilities one level higher than their teachers, epitomizing their exceptional aptitude for surpassing the skills imparted by their mentors. This quality serves as a testament to their unwavering pursuit of excellence and their relentless commitment to pushing the boundaries of their abilities.

## Fate

- Brother of the Bells - You derive the full effects from being a member of the Church of the Bells. Take +1D on all Status tests, with believers. 30% of your Coin must be tied to the church. Finally, you gain an additional benefit based on your order. - The Crown: Add the number of bonus dice assigned to Stewardship to all Persuasion test results. - The Clapper : Can get the first level of any Martial Skill (Merit) regardless of dependencies. - The Mouth : Add the number of bonus dice assigned to Knowledge to Survival Tests. OR  You gain the Sorcerous Initiation benefit for free.
- Man of the Peacekeepers - You gain the full benefits for being a member of the Peacekeepers. It is vital to understand that you are not "local police" you are imperial police think like the FBI, or Federal Marshals. More of the Jedi or UN Peacekeepers. You gain the following benefits: - Increase your Status to 5. (It can only be raised by the Emperor) - Increase your Composure by 2. - When fighting to protect the Imperial Law (while by yourself from Imperial Representees), or the noble family +2 to all Fighting test results. - You also are given a Heirloom Level Long Sword (+1 to hit, +1 damage) [if this is lost you lose your title and effects until you regain it], This is also your badge of office. - You can request ANYWHERE in the kingdom to be quared in any home for one day. They must feed you and give you a roof. After one day you must find another place unless they ask you to stay. - While being a Peacekeeper you gain 1/2 the glory other players would. As the Imperial honor takes its due. - You have one free destiny to burn ONLY to stop you from dying. (this refreshes once a year) - If wearing armor none of your traits work. But you do not lose your titles you just can't push it when wearing armor.
- The Tested - You have undergone the 5 tests of becoming a full fleged member of the tested. You have the following perks! This also means you can not have children AT ALL. - You add your ranks in Cunning to any Knowlge or Will Test. - If Lowborn: You are now linked to the house fortunes of where you are assianged or the Imperieral House. (You must pick this for every promotion, and can not change it until you are promoted.) - if Highborn: Can spend one more Desntiny to take from both the Imperial and their born house. - Your Minium status is a 3. It is considered you are resting on the laurles of your Imperial Rank. The Emperor himself can rase this up to 6.
