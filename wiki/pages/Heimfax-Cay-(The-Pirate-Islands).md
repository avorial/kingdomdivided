# Cities

## Fasingster

Fasingster stands as a bustling haven for buccaneers and brigands alike. Serving as the capital, this "free city" is a hub of intrigue, deals, and alliances among the high seas' most audacious characters. Governed by a council comprising the nine most influential pirates or their proxies, Fasingster's power dynamics are as tempestuous as the oceans surrounding it. Only those who command their own ships earn the right to vote, making seafaring prowess the currency of authority.

The cityscape is a testament to the eclectic lives of pirates from every corner of the realm. Taverns echo with raucous tales of daring escapades, while markets brim with exotic goods plundered from distant shores. Fasingster's chaotic beauty is evident in its maze-like alleys, adorned with flags and sigils of pirate crews. With every tide, ships from around the world anchor in its harbors, forming an ever-shifting tapestry of alliances, rivalries, and the promise of riches untold.

## The Council of the Sea

While there are many groups of independent pirates sailing the seas of the Heathstone, at Fasingster a collocation of pirates meet. This group is brought together by mutual interests with some pirates leaving the group and others joining. Seniority on the council is gained by having a large group of Captains say they support you with their vote and their proxy.  Every person who is the Capitan of a boat, (regardless of it's owner) has one vote in the meetings. This is mostly ceremonial as the standing “Captains” have proxies of many ships, but it still allows the captain the ability to speak before a council. All other speakers must be backed by a person with a ship.  Once every 5 years a census is made of every ship that comes in one calendar year, each ship is given a vote.

### They hold 3 laws sacrosanct

1. A pirate will not take more from his booty then the share he signed on with.
2. A pirate will aid another pirate vs all others as long as his own life would not be in extreme peril.
3. A pirate must follow his Capitan in all orders, until a call for a vote of no confidence. If the vote is not for the Capitan the one who called the vote picks the new Capitan.

## Garingin

Garingin, a storied port of historical significance, is under the reign of House Rameriz, a lineage steeped in piracy's tradition. This is where the legendary pirate Emir Rameriz, the founder of House Silverridge, took his first steps. The cobblestone streets and sea-battered buildings bear the weight of countless tales, echoing with the exploits of generations past.

House Rameriz's legacy is etched in every ship's hull and every glint of treasure. The port's architecture tells of daring raids, fierce sea battles, and audacious escapades that have shaped the very core of the pirate isle. Garingin's bustling docks and sprawling shipyards bear witness to the ongoing saga of House Silverridge, a constant reminder of the tenacity that has marked the Rameriz name throughout history.

## Mastmend

Nestled amid the craggy coastline, Mastmend emerges as an unexpected tropical oasis within the otherwise chilly expanse of the pirate island. Its unique allure draws sailors from distant Mikado Islands and Saltbloods seeking respite from their maritime adventures. Built around geothermal springs, the city boasts a climate that contrasts sharply with its surrounding environs. Lush palm trees and vibrant flowers flourish, their vivid hues a stark contrast to the grey skies and turbulent seas.

Mastmend's architecture echoes its exotic ambiance, with colorful buildings adorned with intricate carvings and vibrant textiles. The city's docks teem with ships of various origins, bearing the tales and wares of diverse lands. A diverse and eclectic culture thrives here, a melting pot of sailors' traditions interwoven with the island's pirate lore. As the sun sets over the tranquil bay, the rhythmic beat of drums and the intoxicating scent of spices fill the air, testament to the city's unique identity as a crossroads of the high seas.

## Quathwent

Amidst the shadowy crags and misty peaks of the pirate island, the sacred city of Quathwent stands as a spiritual haven for those who brave its treacherous paths. Pirates embark on pilgrimages to invoke the blessings of luck and curse their adversaries in equal measure. This mystical place is guarded by the Esten, revered as the keepers of ancient rituals and knowledge of the old gods. Intricate temples carved into the mountainside beckon to those seeking communion with powers beyond the mortal realm.

Quathwent's atmosphere is thick with the scent of incense and salt as pilgrims engage in sacred rites, their voices carrying their hopes and grievances to the heavens. The city's enigmatic aura is heightened by the perpetual shroud of fog that blankets its streets, obscuring the line between reality and the supernatural. Ancient tomes and cryptic artifacts are traded in hidden markets, and the sound of ritual drums echoes through labyrinthine alleys. Quathwent's power transcends the material, drawing pirates to its mysterious embrace in their quest for otherworldly intervention.

## Willford

Jutting defiantly from the icy northern waters, the desolate isle of Willford stands as a testament to the island's harsh realities. Bereft of trees and warmth, the island's inhabitants eke out a survival that mirrors the isolation of their surroundings. A resilient and self-sufficient community has emerged, with fishing and clamming providing sustenance, while the dank hovels yield precious mushrooms, a meager source of nourishment.

Life on Willford is a dance with solitude and the spirits of the departed. The wind's mournful howls and the endless waves form a haunting symphony that envelops the island's shores. Those who have lived on Willford long enough whisper of the company kept by the island's dead, their ethereal presence believed to offer solace in the otherwise desolate expanse. It's a place where the lines between life and death, reality and memory, blur into an eerie tableau that shapes the island's unique character.
