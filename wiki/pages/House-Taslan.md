### **House Taslan**

**Symbol:** An open book with bricks behind it.  
**Words:** "Through History, We Endure"

House Taslan is a house steeped in the ancient traditions of knowledge and secrecy. Known as the spymasters, librarians, and chroniclers of the Esten people, they are the custodians of a legacy that predates even the esteemed Esten Kings. House Taslan is dedicated to preserving the magical heritage that is slowly fading from the world, and they are determined to use the wisdom of the past to pave the way for the resurgence of the Esten Kings.

#### **History and Mission**

House Taslan's roots run deep into the annals of history. Long before the fall of the Esten Kings, the Taslan were already revered as keepers of secrets and knowledge. Their vast libraries contained scrolls and tomes filled with arcane lore, historical records, and forbidden magics. During the reign of the Esten Kings, they served as invaluable advisors, their insights shaping the policies and strategies of the realm.

The cataclysmic fall of the Esten Kings marked a period of great upheaval for House Taslan. As the invaders ravaged the land, the Taslan focused on preserving their vast stores of knowledge, smuggling precious texts and artifacts to hidden sanctuaries. This era of turmoil solidified their role as the unseen guardians of the Esten legacy, a role they have embraced with unwavering dedication. Regardless of all the good they had done, they are seen as the failures of the esten people, for in all their wisdom and vision they did not lead the people to victory they have led them to shame and loss.

#### **Culture and Characteristics**

House Taslan is defined by its relentless pursuit of knowledge and its mastery of subterfuge. The members of this house are meticulous scholars, skilled spies, and adept chroniclers. Their libraries are unparalleled, containing rare and ancient texts that cover a myriad of subjects, from the mundane to the mystical. Every member who joins the house has the uncanny ability to read at the same time they learn to speak, it is the mark of this house. (PC Players of this house must be able to read)

**Traditions:**

- **The Red Spot Vigil:** An annual event where members of House Taslan gather to reflect on their history and the knowledge they have gathered. The red spot on their symbol represents both the blood shed in the pursuit of knowledge and the focal point of their vision for the future. During this vigil, they read from the ancient texts and renew their commitment to their mission.
- **The Serpent's Bite:** A rite of passage for young Taslan initiates. They must navigate a labyrinth filled with traps and illusions, representing the winding and often perilous path to true knowledge. At the heart of the labyrinth lies a hidden text, which they must decipher and present to the elders as proof of their readiness to join the ranks of the house.

#### **Current Standing**

House Taslan holds a unique and influential position among the remnants of the Esten Empire. Their vast network of spies and informants provides them with unparalleled access to information, allowing them to influence events from the shadows. They are respected and feared for their ability to uncover secrets and manipulate outcomes to serve their long-term goals.

Their libraries, though hidden and often mobile, remain a treasure trove of knowledge sought by scholars and rulers alike. House Taslan's chroniclers continue to document the present, weaving it into the rich tapestry of history that they guard so jealously. Their ultimate goal is to find a way to restore the Esten Kings to their former glory, using the wisdom of the past to guide the path forward.

In summary, House Taslan is a house of guardians, spies, and scholars. Their dedication to preserving and utilizing the knowledge of a bygone era sets them apart as the custodians of the Esten legacy. Through their meticulous chronicling and strategic maneuvering, they strive to rekindle the flame of the Esten Kings and ensure that their history is not forgotten, but rather, reborn.
