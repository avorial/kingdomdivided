In the annals of history, the origins of the Crusader Wars, a tumultuous era that fractured the unity of the Constellation of Stars, are often obscured by the passage of time. Yet, within the labyrinth of forgotten narratives, there lies a tale that sparked the flames of conflict—a tale centered around a dismissive teenager named Emanuel Gratsi and the consequences that rippled through the fabric of a society that considered itself untouchable.

Emanuel Gratsi, heir to the illustrious House Gratsi and the eldest of four siblings, bore the weight of privilege with the nonchalance typical of youth. His days in school were marked by popularity and affluence, making him the object of many admirations. Among his admirers was Olivia Hendricks, a member of House Hendricks, which served as a banner to the Ducal Gratsi. Olivia harbored an intense infatuation with Emanuel, and her aspirations took a fateful turn when she formally approached him with a request for marriage.

Emanuel, blinded by arrogance and entitlement, cruelly dismissed Olivia's proposal. In his laughter, he cemented the social hierarchy that dictated such unions were beneath a duke's lineage. Heartbroken, Olivia's infatuation transformed into an obsession. Unyielding in her belief that she could alter Emanuel's perception, she began to shadow him, watching and waiting for an opportunity to change his heart.

Her chance came when she witnessed Emanuel sneaking into a villa. Scaling the ivy-covered walls, she clandestinely observed him engaging in a liaison with a lady's maid. Consumed by jealousy and rage, Olivia's sanity snapped. Bursting through the window, she seized a nearby statue and, in a fit of madness, bludgeoned them both to death. In the aftermath, she lingered in the blood-stained room, awaiting the discovery of her heinous crime.

When Albrech Gratsi, Emanuel's father and faithful to the Constellation of Stars, learned of the tragedy, he confronted the priests of the constellation. Fueled by grief and anger, he demanded to know why the priests had not foreseen and prevented this calamity that befell his house. The silence that met his queries intensified his wrath, casting the entire priesthood under his condemnation.

Amidst Albrech's cries, devoid of divine comfort, the bell rang. The Bellringer, an enigmatic force whose presence intertwined fate and reality, spoke directly to Albrech. The revelation was profound—it was not ignorance on the part of the Constellation of Stars that led to Emanuel's demise; rather, it was a deliberate act by its members who sought to humble the nobility. The Bellringer, restrained by the will of his brothers, yearned to warn him of the impending tragedy but found himself shackled.

In his emotionally shattered state, Albrech Gratsi found purpose in the voice of the Bellringer. The bell's resonance transformed him into a crusader, a man with a mission bestowed upon him by forces beyond mortal comprehension. When Albrech shared this divine revelation with his peers, many dismissed him as a grief-stricken lunatic. Only one man, Luther Convernguard, perceived the gravity of Albrech's words and recognized the potential for upheaval.

Augustus and Luther, united by the call of the Bellringer, embarked on a journey to prepare for a war that would redefine the balance of power within the Constellation of Stars. The whisper of the bell echoed through their ranks, a prelude to the chaos that awaited—a chaos that would be known as the Crusader Wars, a testament to the profound consequences that followed the dismissal of a teenager's affections.
