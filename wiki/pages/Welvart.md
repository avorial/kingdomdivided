# History

The Welvart, a once-oppressed race of slaves originating from the great city of Kadal, found themselves at the crossroads of history. Hailing from diverse backgrounds, the Welvart were a hardworking and resilient community that had been dispersed across the expansive Kadalian empire. Over time, their unique position as slaves in various regions allowed them to master the hidden languages of the diverse peoples under Kadalian rule.

Within Welvart families, the art of communication took on a fascinating dimension. Welvart children, as a matter of course, engaged in the creation of secret languages within their social circles. This linguistic prowess became a defining characteristic, positioning the Welvart as indispensable assets capable of bridging communication gaps between the ruling elite and the diverse populations scattered throughout the empire.

The Welvart's linguistic talents became instrumental in the functioning of the Kadalian bureaucracy. Spread to the far corners of the domain, Welvart individuals served as invaluable translators, facilitating communication between the ruling class and the subjects under Kadal's oppressive thumb. From humble roles like money changers to prestigious positions as governors in distant domains, the Welvart had become an integral part of all levels of Kadalian bureaucracy.

While the Welvart were never elevated to the status of nobility, they held a crucial role in maintaining the functionality of the empire. Their ability to navigate the intricate web of languages and cultures within the Kadalian domain ensured a degree of cohesion that might have otherwise eluded the sprawling empire. The Welvart were not only linguistic conduits but also diplomats, mediators, and administrators who played a vital role in the smooth operation of the Kadalian system.

However, the stability and necessity of the Welvart's role were abruptly disrupted by the catastrophic event known as the Heartbeat. As the seas boiled crimson and the mountains seemed to flee from their foundations, the very fabric of the empire unraveled. The networks that had relied on the Welvart as linguistic bridges crumbled in the face of environmental upheaval. In this moment of crisis, survival instincts kicked in, and the Welvart were forced to adapt to the changing circumstances.

With the empire in disarray and the once-stable bureaucratic structures shattered, the Welvart found themselves at a crossroads. The need for linguistic mastery took a backseat to the urgent demands of survival. As people grappled with the immediate challenges of securing food and shelter in the wake of the Heartbeat, the Welvart, once essential bureaucrats, underwent a transformation.

Faced with the necessity to adapt or perish, the Welvart turned to trade and commerce as a means of survival. The once-silent merchants and traders emerged from the shadows, their linguistic talents now serving a different purpose – facilitating transactions and negotiations in the chaotic aftermath of the Heartbeat. No longer confined to the bureaucratic structures that had defined their existence, the Welvart became resourceful entrepreneurs navigating a world reshaped by cataclysmic events.

The transition from bureaucratic facilitators to merchants and traders marked a profound shift in the Welvart's identity. They had once been the linchpin holding together the Kadalian empire, and now they found themselves forging new paths in a world where the old structures had crumbled. The Welvart, once instrumental in upholding the imperial order, now became architects of their destinies as they ventured into the uncertain landscape of post-Heartbeat survival.

In this new era, Welvart communities established trade routes, negotiated deals, and navigated the complexities of a world transformed. The linguistic skills that had once been wielded to maintain the imperial bureaucracy now became tools for building economic networks and establishing connections with other surviving communities.

# Stereotypes

## Biological traits

They are shorter on average with the common height being 5'5. They have black or brown hair.

## Language

The Welvart speak all languages, never as a native but always with a refined bookishness that comes from knowing the core of all speech.
