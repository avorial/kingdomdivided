# History

In the aftermath of the Heartbeat, a cataclysm that sent shockwaves through the world, survivors found themselves in the distant lands situated to the southwest. These lands, far removed from the once-prosperous empires like Kadal, they bore the scars of devastation more profoundly. Unlike their counterparts in more resource-abundant regions, the inhabitants of these exiled territories struggled to rebuild, lacking the necessary means to pull themselves out of the ashes.

As communities began to form in this new frontier, a natural hierarchy emerged. Certain individuals displayed a remarkable aptitude for learning and adapting to the challenges of the post-Heartbeat world more quickly than others. These exceptional individuals were soon elected as leaders within their respective tribes, entrusted with the responsibility of guiding the construction of new cities and fostering a sense of unity among the survivors.

However, the initial spirit of cooperation and shared purpose began to wane over time. Human nature, with its intrinsic tendency towards greed and power, led to a fracture among the once-unified tribes. Civil unrest escalated, with conflicts arising between brothers who had once stood shoulder to shoulder in the face of adversity. The dream of rebuilding civilization seemed to be slipping away, replaced by the specter of internal strife that threatened to plunge the people back into the bleak era of ash.

Amidst this turmoil, a seemingly inconspicuous figure emerged from the shadows – a humble stone polisher by the name of Kato Leandan. Unassuming in appearance, Kato possessed a wisdom that transcended his station. He felt compelled to address the collective consciousness of the fractured tribes and impart a vision that could rekindle the flames of hope.

Gathering the disheartened groups, Kato delivered stirring speeches that resonated with a profound sense of purpose. He advocated not just for survival but for a more enlightened way of living. Kato's core message emphasized the connection to others within the community. To protect oneself and the fruits of one's labor, Kato argued, it was imperative to first protect one's brother. This philosophy struck a chord with those who listened, offering a glimmer of hope in the darkest of times.

Kato envisioned a society where hard work and the intentional shaping of the world would yield not just survival, but prosperity. He spoke of a collective bounty, where all individuals could rise together in the tide of industry. His words resonated deeply with those who longed for a return to the ideals of cooperation and shared progress.

The impact of Kato's speeches was profound. They ignited a transformative movement that united the disparate tribes under a common republic. Those who aligned themselves with Kato's vision and had a heritage to him became known as Katoites, a community bound by shared values and a commitment to the principles espoused by their visionary leader.

In the wake of this newfound unity, the tribes, once torn apart by internal strife, coalesced into a cohesive republic. This collective of tribes adopted the name of their inspirational leader, forming the Leandan League. The League became a symbol of resilience, rebirth, and the triumph of shared ideals over the divisive forces that had threatened to tear them asunder.

The Leandan League embarked on a journey of reconstruction. New cities rose from the ruins, reflecting not only physical resilience but also the indomitable spirit of a people who had chosen unity over discord. The philosophy of mutual protection and collaborative industry became the cornerstone of their societal structure.

# Stereotypes

## Biological traits

They are taller on average with the common height being 5'10. They are pale, pinkish and vulnerable to sunburns. Blond or fair-haired.

## Language

It is a flowing language with intensity, many stirring speeches are thought best to be first written in Leandan.
