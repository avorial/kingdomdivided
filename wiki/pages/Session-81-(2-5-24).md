# Synopsis

In Game 81, the characters wake up and learn about bandit attacks on foreigners, with a specific group of bandits having black and red hair. Lord Quangbang shares information about the raids, revealing that 30 victims were attacked, including 10 Saltblood and 20 foreigners, but the Mikkado were not among them. Togo provides additional details about the bandits, noting their cleanliness, possession of unused items, and the presence of turkeys native to Belkind.

The bandits wore reversed cloaks, indicating an attempt to hide something. Notably, a bandit with poor fighting skills had water on his face, which turned out to be glasses. Lord Quangbang patrols the roads in response.

Later, the group discusses the need for Peacekeepers and reveals a sighting of a knight from House Rhelrya killing a Montimier knight. The decision not to request more peacekeepers is based on concerns for their safety. The characters consider the possibility of baiting the bandits for more information.

The group then tracks the bandit attacks, heading south towards Shelby. On the way, they encounter a dragon attack at an archaeological dig site, where bandits had been capturing foreigners as forced labor. They find evidence suggesting the bandits were connected to House Rhelrya and were trying to prevent the spread of information about stopping kaiju attacks.

Julien discovers a lay line in the archaeological dig, linking it to Seline. The group encounters the leader of the city of Buon Nguyen, Sir Keto Tamara, who is being taken over by a dark reflection. The city, inhabited by various races, heavily relies on obsidian mirrors for protection. The characters catch a boat down the river as they continue their journey.
