## [¶](#house-jeong) **House Jeong**

**Title:** The Silk Roaders  
**Location:** Port of Xunzhen and the Inner Market Chain  
**Faith:** Ancestor spirits (blended with Worm God reverence)  
**Role:** Commerce, diplomacy, soft power projection

### [¶](#overview) **Overview**

House Jeong is the commercial lifeblood of the Empire, a dynasty of negotiators, merchants, and cultural brokers. Their fleet of trade junks and caravans reaches every major city and border, and their influence seeps into every court through coin, contract, or convenience.

They prefer to trade favors rather than threats, and many see them as the *true* rulers of the empire’s future—if not its present. Their elite children are raised as polyglots, trained in diplomacy, and adorned with silks imported from beyond the Salt Dunes.

### [¶](#leadership) **Leadership**

**Grand Consul Ren Jeong**  
A silver-tongued patriarch who has outlived three Emperors through skillful neutrality and clever alliances. He dresses plainly but speaks with the weight of ten thousand coins. His motto is: “Peace buys better margins.”

**Key Members**

**Consul Hei Jeong**  
Ren’s daughter and master of the Jeong trading fleet. Hei is known for both her shrewdness and her generosity, often resolving disputes with lavish gifts. Beneath her gentle demeanor is a calculating tactician.

**Broker-General Fin Jeong**  
A former spy turned diplomat, Fin handles international relations. He often wears disguises and is fluent in eight languages, including the divine tongue used in ancient rituals.

**Silk-Chanter Nian Jeong**  
A poet and socialite who entertains nobles at court with musical stories woven with economic advice. Many policies have been changed after one of her “performances.”
