# Star Gods

The Star Gods are beings that originated from the tears shed by O during the creation of the world. They are associated with celestial forces, cosmic powers, and the mysteries of the universe. The most prominent Star God in the story is the Bellringer, who holds a central role and is revered as a divine figure. The Bellringer and other Star Gods may possess abilities and knowledge beyond mortal comprehension, and their worshipers seek their favor and guidance. Each god has a open side and a closed side, some religions focus on one or the other, rarely both.

- Bellringer: The Exiled Luminary - (A Bell) The Bellringer constellation, once a prominent figure among the Star Gods, now resides on the fringes of the celestial tapestry. Its luminous presence serves as a reminder of a fallen deity, symbolizing the repercussions of arrogance and the pursuit of dominance. The Bellringer's followers, once revered, were cast out by a coalition of other Star Gods who sought to maintain balance and harmony among the celestial realms.
- Aurora: The Dawnbringer - (A Symbol of a Sun) Aurora, a steadfast advocate of celestial harmony, played a crucial role in the Bellringer's expulsion. Her constellation shines with renewed brilliance, signifying the triumph of unity over individual ambition. Aurora and her loyal followers banded together, guiding the celestial council's decision to sever ties with the Bellringer and his followers, ensuring that the divine order remained undisturbed.
- Nyxaris: The Midnight Guardian - (2 Blades) Nyxaris, the ever-watchful protector of celestial balance, stood resolute in the face of the Bellringer's attempts to assert dominance. With the might of the constellation behind him, Nyxaris joined forces with other Star Gods to confront the Bellringer and his followers. Their collective strength ensured that the delicate equilibrium of the celestial realm remained intact.
- Solara: The Solar Sovereign - (A Crown) Solara, the embodiment of cosmic wisdom, recognized the Bellringer's misguided ambitions and its potential to disrupt the celestial harmony. The Solara constellation stands as a beacon of enlightenment, guiding the divine council in their decision to expel the Bellringer's followers. Solara's followers, ever devoted to the pursuit of knowledge, played a vital role in restoring order among the stars.
- Lunastra: The Lunar Luminary - (Glowing “Moon” rocks) Lunastra, with her deep connection to the ebb and flow of celestial energies, sensed the imbalance caused by the Bellringer's thirst for power. As her constellation glows softly in the night sky, Lunastra supported the decision to cast out the Bellringer's followers, safeguarding the delicate equilibrium between darkness and light within the celestial realm.
- Cometia: The Celestial Wanderer - (a compass) Cometia, the eternal seeker of cosmic truths, traversed the vast expanses of the night sky, witnessing the Bellringer's attempts to rise above the other Star Gods. Her constellation's journey serves as a reminder of the importance of humility and the dangers of unchecked ambition. Cometia's followers played a crucial role in revealing the Bellringer's true intentions, ensuring his expulsion from the celestial pantheon.
- Stellaris: The Cosmic Weaver - (A Spool of Thread) Stellaris, the master weaver of cosmic connections, wove intricate patterns of diplomacy and consensus among the Star Gods. The Stellaris constellation sparkles with interwoven threads of celestial unity, reminding all who gaze upon it of the necessity for collaboration and cooperation. It was Stellaris' guidance that led to the decision to sever ties with the Bellringer and his followers, preserving the harmonious interplay of the divine realms.

# Worm Gods

The Worm Gods are a pantheon of deities associated with darkness, corruption, destruction, mysteries and healthy change. They are believed to have played a role in the demise of O, bringing about chaos and decay from within. Their followers are predominantly found in the Mikado Empire, a southern region of the world in "Kingdom Divided." The Worm Gods are often portrayed as malevolent entities, and their worship involves rituals and practices centered around the darker aspects of existence.

In the world of "Kingdom Divided," it is believed that the most faithful followers of the Worm Gods, the pantheon associated with darkness and destruction, have the potential to undergo a transformation into anthropomorphic animals. This phenomenon occurs as a result of a deep connection and devotion to the Worm Gods, where their influence transcends the boundaries of mortal forms.

When individuals are deemed exceptionally loyal and committed to the worship of the Worm Gods, they may undergo a spiritual or physical metamorphosis. Through this transformation, they acquire animalistic features, taking on the characteristics and traits of various creatures. This change reflects their alignment with the darker forces and their assimilation of aspects associated with the animal kingdom.

The exact nature of these transformations can vary, as followers of the Worm Gods may manifest different animal forms depending on their personal devotion or the specific deity they align with. These anthropomorphic animals often possess heightened senses, physical attributes, or abilities reminiscent of their animal counterparts.

The transformation into anthropomorphic animals is seen as a sign of the favor bestowed upon the most devout worshipers by the Worm Gods. It marks their status as chosen vessels or intermediaries between the divine and mortal realms. These individuals may serve as priests, champions, or agents of the Worm Gods, carrying out their will and spreading their influence in the world.

The existence of these transformed beings adds another layer of mysticism and complexity to the religious beliefs and practices surrounding the Worm Gods in "Kingdom Divided." It highlights the intertwining of spirituality and physical manifestation, blurring the boundaries between human and animal forms in the context of faith and divine connection.

# Old Gods

The Old Gods represent the ancient deities associated with nature and natural phenomena. They embody the elemental forces of the world, such as thunder, lava, forests, and other aspects of the environment. These gods are deeply connected to the land, and their worshipers seek their blessings for bountiful harvests, protection, and balance in the natural order. The Old Gods are revered as guardians and caretakers of the world's ecosystems, and their followers often engage in rituals and ceremonies that honor and appease these powerful entities.

- Toprak Ana, the Soil Mother: Toprak Ana is the goddess of the soil and fertility. She embodies the nurturing and life-giving qualities of the earth. Followers of Toprak Ana believe that she grants abundant harvests, protects the soil, and ensures the prosperity of the people. Offerings to Toprak Ana often include seeds, grains, and items associated with agriculture, symbolizing the bountiful gifts of the land.
