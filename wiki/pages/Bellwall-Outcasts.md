The roar of the seas seemed to echo the tumultuous nature of the Crusader Wars against the Mikado Empire. The conflict had persisted for ages, a relentless struggle between powers vying for religious dominance. Ten years ago, amidst the ebb and flow of this ceaseless war, a defining moment unfolded—a clandestine operation known as the Burning of Ganzhou.

At the heart of this audacious endeavor was a group of unlikely heroes or, perhaps more aptly, criminals given a chance at redemption. Drawn from the ranks of ruffians and prisoners of Bellwall, this motley crew faced a stark choice: fight under the banner of the Bellringer for a chance at freedom or meet their end for the crimes that had condemned them. The leader of this disparate unit bore the name Kaelen Blackfin, a name that would soon become synonymous with both infamy and, surprisingly, a twisted form of heroism.

## Kaelen Blackfin:

Leader of the Bellwall Outcasts, Kaelen was a former pirate captain with a fearsome reputation. He had terrorized the trade routes of the Bell Lands, plundering and pillaging with ruthless efficiency. The Bellringer's offer, presented in the shadow of the gallows, was a chance for Kaelen to exchange his noose for a sword. Guided by an insistent ringing in his ears, a constant reminder of the Bellringer's watchful gaze, Kaelen accepted the offer. His own history was intertwined with the very seas he sailed, and he saw this mission as a way to repay a debt to the Bellringer.

## Thessa "Rat-Eyes" Mercer:

Once a skilled thief and assassin, Thessa had navigated the murky underworld of Oldwyn's criminal network. She earned her moniker "Rat-Eyes" for her ability to slip through the darkest corners unnoticed. Captured by the imperial authorities, she faced the choice of a swift execution or joining the Bellwall Outcasts. Under the keen gaze of Kaelen Blackfin, Thessa chose the latter. Her nimble fingers and stealthy movements made her a vital asset in the shadows of Ganzhou.

## Darius "Redhands" Garnett:

A former enforcer for a notorious crime syndicate in Bellwall, Darius had left a trail of brutality in his wake. Known as "Redhands" for the blood that stained his palms, he was a man of few words and unbridled violence. Arrested after a failed attempt to take control of his syndicate, Darius faced the gallows. The Bellringer's offer became his salvation, and he became the muscle of the Bellwall Outcasts, a silent force ready to unleash chaos upon Ganzhou.

## Isolde "Shroudcloak" Ravenshadow:

A skilled sorceress with a dark past, Isolde had been a Odd Fellows that dabbled in forbidden magics. Her ambitions led her to perform rituals that drew the attention of the Peacekeepers. Arrested and charged with practicing dark arts, Isolde faced a grim fate. The Bellringer, recognizing the potential of her arcane abilities, offered her a chance at redemption. Under the alias "Shroudcloak," Isolde weaved shadows and mystic energies to aid the Outcasts in their mission.

## Harold "Ironfist" Riggs:

Once a pit fighter celebrated for his brutal victories, Harold's temper and pride led him to defy the criminal overlords who controlled the fighting pits of Covenguard. His rebellion was short-lived, and he found himself facing the hangman's noose. The Bellringer's intervention spared him, and Harold, now known as "Ironfist," brought his combat prowess to the Outcasts. His iron fists and indomitable spirit made him a formidable ally.

The Burning of Ganzhou was not a straightforward assault on the Mikado forces; it was a calculated infiltration that relied on cunning and misdirection. The Bellwall Outcasts, disguised as Worsouk Whalers, sailed a junk into Ganzhou harbor. Merging seamlessly with the city's populace, they became shadows in the midst of chaos.

Over weeks, the Outcasts surreptitiously stockpiled whale oil, turning the city into a ticking inferno. Their plan was to unleash a wave of fires, each igniting as the Mikado authorities struggled to contain the previous one. It was a dance of chaos orchestrated by the Bellwall Outcasts, a symphony of destruction with Ganzhou as its stage.

Yet, their mission was not merely one of devastation. To elevate their audacious act, the Outcasts captured Hariko Toab, the third in line to the esteemed Toab Clan. Hariko became both a pawn and a symbol, a captive of the Bellringer's justice.

Southgriffon, standing as a stark emblem of Imperial authority, witnessed the trial of Hariko Toab. The Bellwall Outcasts, now deeply embedded within the fabric of Ganzhou, orchestrated a dramatic spectacle of justice. The accused noble, bound by chains and the weight of his lineage, faced the accusations brought forth by the unlikely agents of the Bellringer.

As the fires raged through Ganzhou, consuming the city in a chaotic blaze, Hariko Toab stood before the judgment of Kaelen Blackfin and his ragtag band. The outcome of this trial would echo through the ages, a testament to the unpredictable machinations set in motion by the Bellringer's unseen hand. The Bellwall Outcasts have become the product of tales all over the Belllands.
