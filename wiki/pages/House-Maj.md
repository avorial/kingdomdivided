## [¶](#house-maj) **House Maj**

**Title:** The Veilwalkers  
**Location:** The Shrine-Hollows of Nyua, Eastern Highlands  
**Faith:** Devout Worm Gods, with unique sectarian rituals  
**Role:** Mystic advisors, guardians of spiritual balance, ritualists

### [¶](#overview) **Overview**

House Maj serves as the arcane soul of the Mikado Empire, a lineage steeped in ancient mystery and spiritual discipline. Their lands are filled with shrines, sacred caves, and ethereal pilgrimage routes that trace back to the time before the Heartbeat. The Maj see themselves not as nobles, but as **custodians of balance**—servants of fate and intercessors between the living and the divine.

Members are marked by glowing brands on their skin—sigils granted during their naming rites by the Worm Gods. They rarely leave their sacred lands, except when summoned to advise the Emperor or interpret omens of crisis.

### [¶](#leadership) **Leadership**

**High Oracle Imai Maj**  
A blind mystic said to “see the future behind the curtain of now.” Imai is rarely seen in public, instead speaking through carved stone avatars animated by whispered chants. She is the longest-reigning leader of House Maj and is rumored to have lived for over 200 years, though no one can verify her age.

**Key Members**

**Ritual Warden Doyan Maj**  
A harsh enforcer of sacred rites, Doyan serves as both guardian and executioner. Those who trespass on Maj ritual grounds rarely survive his judgment. His golden mask and silence make him feared across the realm.

**Mist-Priestess Teliya Maj**  
Young and compassionate, Teliya often travels beyond Maj lands as an emissary of peace and healing. Some nobles accuse her of sorcery, but others trust her word as law when it comes to interpreting omens.

**Scrollspeaker Bael Maj**  
A wandering historian-priest who records divine events with flesh-bound ink on scrolls made of moth wings. His prophecies are often cryptic but have proven eerily accurate.
