## [¶](#house-haina) **House Haina**

**Title:** The Guardian House  
**Current Role:** Royal Guards and Defenders of the Imperial Bloodline  
**Location:** The Glass Spire Barracks, Imperial Inner City  
**Faith:** The Worm Gods (with focus on sacred oaths and ancestral bindings)  
**Role:** Elite protectors, enforcers of imperial security

### [¶](#overview) **Overview**

House Haina is the silent blade in the dark, the unblinking eyes of the throne. Every generation of Hainas is raised from birth to defend the Mikado bloodline. They are bound by unbreakable oaths that prevent them from ever bearing ambition for political power—making them uniquely trusted in a realm full of schemers.

They wear armor forged from volcanic glass and silksteel, and they speak only in the ancient tongue when on duty. When a Haina steps into a room, silence follows—and sometimes fear. Even nobles bow in their presence, for they are more than warriors: they are the living law of the Emperor.

### [¶](#leadership) **Leadership**

**High Commander Daigon Haina**  
A stoic and towering presence, Daigon leads the Royal Guard with militant precision. It is said he has not smiled since the day he took the Oath of Service. Rumors swirl that he once single-handedly stopped an assassination attempt on the Emperor by hurling a table through a wall.

**Matron-Commander Shiha Haina**  
Daigon’s sister, she trains the next generation of royal guards. Known for using ancient salamander-tactics that combine silence, motion, and spiritual presence. Students say training with her is like fighting an avalanche.

### [¶](#key-members) **Key Members**

**Captain Vileh Haina**  
A gifted scout and intelligence handler, Vileh operates among the other houses as a “silent messenger.” Her words are considered binding truths in court, and no one knows the full extent of her intelligence network.

**Initiate Jiku Haina**  
A rare prodigy, Jiku joined the guard at only 13 years of age. Now 17, she is whispered to be the Emperor’s personal shadow—unseen and never far behind.
