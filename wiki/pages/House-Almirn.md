## History

The first Saltbloods settled in the Bell Lands because of a bargain struck during the First Bell Ringer Crusade.

The crusading armies lacked the wealth necessary to transport all of their soldiers, supplies, and settlers across the seas. The Saltblood captains offered a solution: they would carry as many crusaders as possible without payment. In exchange, once the war was won, they would receive one hundred square miles of land for every free shipment transported.

When Ethan Sedi was crowned king, the Saltbloods arrived to claim what they were owed.

Their spokesman, Panuk Almirn, appeared before the new monarch carrying a collection of captains' logs. Each log contained the marks and signatures of Bell Ringer officers who had called upon the pact during the crusade.

Ethan Sedi thanked Panuk and retired with his council.

The discussion that followed nearly erupted into chaos. For sixty years the Bell Ringers had fought and died to conquer the Bell Lands. Entire generations had shed blood for every mile of territory. Now vast portions of that hard-won land would have to be surrendered to outsiders, many of whom the nobility still viewed as heretics.

Ethan listened to every argument in silence.

When the room finally fell quiet, he delivered a speech that would become famous throughout the kingdom. He declared that he would "cut the fish in half." Rather than making the Saltbloods subjects of the nobility, he would make them nobles themselves.

The final agreement granted fifty square miles for each voyage rather than one hundred. In return, Panuk Almirn was elevated to the rank of Duke and given authority to distribute lands and titles among the other Saltblood captains.

Though some nobles still resent that the Saltbloods did not personally fight for the Bell Ringer cause, House Almirn has remained loyal to the Crown and Empire for centuries.

Secrets

Many old noble families still quietly question the legitimacy of House Almirn's lands, arguing that they were purchased with contracts rather than won through blood and sacrifice.

The oldest Saltblood records may contain evidence that the original agreement promised far more territory than was ultimately granted by Ethan Sedi.

Certain factions within House Almirn believe the family should reclaim what was lost during the negotiations and restore the full Saltblood inheritance.
