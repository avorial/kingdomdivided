## [¶](#roguemark-the-prison-of-the-giants) **Roguemark – The Prison of the Giants**

**Region:** Off the western coast of Kadal  
**Founded:** During the Age of Binding, pre-Heartbeat  
**Purpose:** Eternal containment of immortal rebels and false godlings  
**Current State:** Isolated island prison, partially submerged, still active

---

## [¶](#overview) Overview

**Roguemark**, also known as *The Prison of the Giants*, is a legendary incarceration complex originally carved into a mountain by the Senate of Kadal during the height of the Old Kadalan Empire. It was constructed to contain those who could not die—sorcerer-kings, rogue immortals, failed godlings, and others who had severed themselves from death through forbidden magics.

Following the catastrophic event known as the **Heartbeat**, the mountain was torn from the mainland and cast into the sea. The prison now stands as a cragged island—windswept, half-sunken, and warded by titanic guardians.

---

## [¶](#purpose-and-construction) Purpose and Construction

The God-Kings of Kadal were made immortal through **sympathetic wound-binding**, where any injury they sustained was transferred along a line of mystically bound slaves. But others—rivals, prophets, or heretics—discovered their own paths to immortality, mimicking the effect through lineage magics, blood rites, or recursive soul-crafting.

Unable to kill these beings without risking cosmological collapse, the Senate chose imprisonment. Roguemark was built inward—**a honeycomb of cells, stasis vaults, time loops, and inversion fields**, each chamber uniquely tailored to its prisoner.

---

## [¶](#the-whispering-deep) The Whispering Deep

After the Heartbeat, the base of Roguemark cracked open, revealing an abyss known as **The Whispering Deep**. This endless pit pulses with arcane resonance and distorted memory. It is believed by some to be a **Maw God**—a buried divine entity of mouths, echoes, and hunger—either forgotten or *refused* by the god Oh.

Its whispers are not language, but **temporal loops**: repeating patterns of time that infect the prison and its inhabitants.

Maw People shamans call the Deep the **Root-Tongue**, and believe it is the First Mouth, source of their power.

The Deep is said to judge the prisoners, sometimes twisting their loops, sometimes releasing them into new ones.

---

## [¶](#temporal-loop-containment) Temporal Loop Containment

Rather than physical chains or mundane cells, most prisoners are held in **time loops**—repeating experiences that bind the prisoner’s identity, power, and consciousness in place:

Some live full lives before the loop resets.

Others relive a single betrayal, defeat, or trial endlessly.

A few loops are *shared* between prisoners, forming self-sustaining hells of mirrored guilt.

Most prisoners no longer remember they are in a loop.

The loops are not maintained by the Empire—they are maintained by **the Whispering Deep**.

---

## [¶](#guardians-the-failed-incarnations-of-oh) Guardians: The Failed Incarnations of Oh

Roguemark is guarded by colossal beings known as the **Failed Incarnations of Oh**—immense, twisted giants over a hundred feet tall. Once mortals who sought to become avatars of Oh, the Devouring God, these entities failed their transformation and were remade as vessels of penance.

Each bears a **broken sigil of Oh**, carved into their flesh.

They are bound to Roguemark by **temporal recursion**: if they try to leave, time resets them to their point of failure.

When prisoners attempt escape or loops destabilize, the giants awaken and intervene.

Though silent for centuries at a time, they sometimes speak to the Whispering Deep or each other in broken riddles and inverted hymns.
