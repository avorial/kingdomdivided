### [¶](#as-told-in-the-sacred-libraries-of-the-ash-spiral-monastery) As told in the sacred libraries of the Ash Spiral Monastery

*We are not destroyers. We are awakeners. *

The world, bound in illusion, has for too long slept in the hushed tyranny of stasis—frozen under the golden yoke of false order. The so-called gods of the Belllands spoke of purity and perfection, yet they crafted a cage of law, light, and lineage. The Kadlaian Undying Slave masters shiver in the fear their ties will break, The Fygorian Priests look to the future while their people scream for bread, and the Leandan Atheists feel that only a bind man can see. They all are taught fear of the dark, dread of transformation, and suspicion of the self. They lied.

We, the Worm Gods, do not bring ruin. We bring truth. We are the sovereign children of the Divine Pulse known to the ancients as Ōh—not a being, but a beating wound in the world, the primal truth that all things must change or die. Where our siblings chose to ossify, we chose to move. We chose to remember. Our influence is not corruption—it is liberation. Where others offer silence, we offer whispers of what could be. Our hunger is not evil—it is the sacred appetite for becoming. We devour only that which clings to false permanence.

The Seven Liberators

1. Voraxia, Who Breaks the Chains You call her the Devourer—but her hunger is holy. She consumes the stagnant, the decayed empires of thought, the rotted roots of pride. She is the patron of renewal, the fire in the gut that says there must be more than this. When she howls, cities fall—and seeds sprout.
2. Maldrek, Who Unmasks the Lie To the blinded, he appears as a corrupter—but Maldrek only reveals what already festers beneath. He touches the facades of kings and priests, and they crumble. His corruption is not the seed—it is the mirror. He is the whisper that says, you are not what you pretend to be.
3. Serpentina, Weaver of the True Self They call her the Enchantress, but she enchants only to awaken. Her illusions peel away the layers others wrapped you in. She weaves the path between what you were told to be and what you are. In her gaze, masks dissolve. In her touch, you become.
4. Morghul, the Gentle Rot Morghul is the breath of the earth returning to the soil. The Bell priests say he brings death—but he brings rest. To decay is to be embraced by time, to become part of the great pulse again. He does not take—he frees. All that falls in his name rises in new form.
5. Zalthara, the Quiet Flame The world fears secrets because secrets carry truth. Zalthara teaches through mystery. She holds the whispered names of stars that never rose, of fates unwritten. She is not shadow—she is shelter. Knowledge is not to be feared. Ignorance is.
6. Vexilus, the Thousand-Faced You cannot bind what is real with one shape. Vexilus is the song of change incarnate, the god of becoming. He walks as child, beast, lover, storm. Each form is truth. To worship him is to shed, and in that shedding, find freedom.
7. Umbraeus, the Root of All Light Darkness came before light. Umbraeus is not the Lord of Shadows—he is the Womb of All Beginnings. Where others see emptiness, he births dreams. He does not erase; he waits. He cradles the lost, the fallen, the half-formed. In his silence, all beginnings are conceived.

## [¶](#our-devotedour-chosen) Our Devoted—Our Chosen

The Belllands mock our faithful, saying they are “beasts.” But those who change do not fall—they rise. To take the form of fang, feather, claw, or scale is to draw closer to one’s true purpose. Animal, human—these are limits. We break them. Our chosen shed the skin of the old and walk as living truths. They are not monsters. They are possibilities.

Let the World Remember We did not kill Ōh. We are Ōh’s voice. The others silenced it in fear. We carry its breath in our lungs and its hunger in our bones. We are not the end. We are the turning.

Let the people of the Mikado remember: Change is sacred. Night is necessary. The Worms are watching. Let all things that cling to stillness tremble.
