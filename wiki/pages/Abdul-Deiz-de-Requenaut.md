# History

Abdul Deiz de Requenaut, an esteemed knight with a storied past, has left an indelible mark on the annals of chivalry. Despite the adversities he has faced, Abdul's resilience and unwavering spirit have defined his legacy.

In his prime, Abdul was a formidable figure on the tournament field, renowned for his skill with a lance and his unwavering determination. He was regarded as one of the most skilled knights of his generation, earning accolades and victories throughout the realm. It was during one fateful joust against his arch-nemesis, Leon Blackwood, that Abdul's life took a dramatic turn.

In the heat of the tournament, Abdul and Leon clashed with thunderous force. Their lances collided, but fate dealt a cruel blow to Abdul. The impact of the strike shattered his armor, causing severe injuries that would forever alter his life. Ribs were wrenched from their rightful place, and his left arm was tragically lost in the violent encounter. It was a devastating blow that forced Abdul to retire from active jousting, but it did not extinguish his spirit.

Before his encounter with Leon, Abdul had taken a young squire named Adan McNeill under his wing. As a mentor and teacher, Abdul imparted his wisdom and honed Adan's skills in the ways of chivalry. Adan, driven by his passion for knighthood, flourished under Abdul's guidance, eventually becoming a capable tourney knight in his own right. The bond between mentor and protégé would forever shape their lives and forge a lasting friendship.

Tragically, years later, Adan met his demise in a joust, once again at the hands of the formidable Leon Blackwood. The loss of his dear protégé fueled Abdul's already burning desire for revenge. Consumed by his thirst to avenge Adan's death, Abdul embarked on a relentless quest, traveling the land in search of a way to defeat his nemesis, Leon.

In his solitary journey, Abdul encountered a mysterious and wise wolf, a creature trained to assist him despite his physical limitation of having only one arm. This loyal companion became Abdul's constant companion and provided invaluable aid and companionship on his quest for vengeance. The wolf's keen senses and instincts proved invaluable, compensating for Abdul's physical shortcomings and bolstering his determination.

Driven by his thirst for retribution and aided by his loyal wolf companion, Abdul Deiz de Requenaut travels the land, seeking the means to finally confront and defeat Leon Blackwood. His journey is one of perseverance, sacrifice, and unwavering determination, as he battles against all odds to restore honor to his fallen protégé and achieve the justice he so passionately seeks.

# Secret

In his early years as a squire, Abdul found himself in a moment of desperation and weakness. He yearned for victory in a crucial tournament, a triumph that would solidify his reputation and bring honor to his name. It was during this time that he made a fateful decision: he struck a forbidden pact with the worm god, Umbraeus. In exchange for his soul, Abdul was granted unparalleled skill, strength, and an indomitable presence on the tournament field.

With the power of the worm god flowing through him, Abdul emerged victorious in the tournament, his prowess on display for all to see. But the euphoria of triumph was short-lived as he realized the grave consequences of his actions. The pact he had made with Umbraeus had bound his fate to the insidious entity, and the malevolent grip of the worm god tightened around his soul.

When the pivotal moment arrived, where Abdul faced his arch-nemesis Leon Blackwood, his newfound skill and strength inexplicably faltered. The dark presence of Umbraeus whispered doubts and tormented his mind, distracting him at the crucial moment. The defeat that followed was a devastating blow, both physically and emotionally, leaving Abdul with a profound sense of failure and the bitter knowledge that he had relied on a dark power to achieve his previous victories.

Haunted by his pact with Umbraeus, Abdul seeks redemption and a way to sever the sinister ties that bind him. The haunting visions and malevolent whispers continue to torment his mind, a constant reminder of the dark path he once walked. He believes that by killing Leon Blackwood without the aid of the worm god's influence, he can prove his worthiness and redeem himself in the eyes of his own soul.

Abdul's journey to break free from the clutches of Umbraeus becomes a deeply personal and dangerous endeavor. He seeks guidance from ancient texts, cryptic seers, and wise sages who may hold the key to undoing the tainted legacy that plagues him. Alongside his loyal wolf companion, whose bond was forged amidst the darkness of his pact, Abdul navigates treacherous paths, confronts twisted creatures, and battles inner demons as he strives to reclaim his honor and cleanse his soul.

As he delves deeper into the mysteries surrounding his pact, Abdul will face moral dilemmas, tests of courage, and the ultimate question of whether redemption is truly possible. The journey to break free from the Tainted Legacy will not only define Abdul's character but also shape the course of his ongoing pursuit of vengeance against Leon Blackwood.
