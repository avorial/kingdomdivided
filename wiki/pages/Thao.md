# History

The Thao people are an integral part of the Mikado Empire's rich tapestry. Their origin story traces back to the momentous event known as "The Final Exchange" when O, the previous incarnation, was defeated by the Worm Gods. During the chaotic upheaval, the Thao emerged as one of the first races of people to break free from the remnants of O. With their memories of the past and the guidance of the Worm Gods, they received advanced knowledge of magic and technology, enabling them to form tribal groups and establish their unique civilization.

Over the centuries, the Thao have cultivated a deep cultural heritage, incorporating ancient traditions and customs into their way of life. Their connection to the Worm Gods and the remnants of O gives them a profound sense of identity and purpose within the Mikado Empire.

# Stereotypes

The Thao are renowned throughout the Mikado Empire as stalwart defenders, embodying unwavering loyalty, indomitable courage, and an unyielding spirit. They are often seen as mighty warriors and scholars, revered for their prowess in both combat and intellectual pursuits. However, these stereotypes can sometimes overshadow the diverse talents and contributions that individuals within the Thao heritage bring to the empire.

# Biological Traits

Physically, the Thao possess distinctive traits that set them apart. They have a thin and lithe build, similar in height to the average person. Their agility, dexterity, and quick reflexes make them formidable in combat, while their keen intellect allows them to think and act swiftly. However, prolonged physical tasks may slow them down, prompting them to seek more efficient solutions through their resourcefulness and cleverness.

# Language

The Thao communicate through a unique language known as Thaonese. Outside the Mikado Empire, it is often referred to as the Mikado Tongue. Thaonese is characterized by its sharp and crisp tones, warm inflections, and intricate expressions. Rooted in ancient stories and cultural cues, Thaonese preserves the wisdom and traditions of the Thao ancestors. It can be challenging for foreigners to learn, as it requires an understanding of the rich historical narratives and cultural nuances embedded within the language.

## Blood of the Thao

The "Blood of the Thao" quality represents the inherent connection to creation that flows through the veins of Thao individuals.

Sword Chronicle - When they spend their last temporary Destiny, they possess a unique ability. By rolling a dice, on a result of 5 or 6, they do not lose their Destiny and can immediately spend it again in the next turn. This ability can only be activated when they are on their last Destiny and applies exclusively to temporary Destiny expenditures. Furthermore, Thao individuals have the privilege of purchasing extra Destiny for 40 XP instead of the usual 50. These additional points, known as "kismet," can be used as Destiny for any purpose except the acquisition of new qualities.

Burning Wheel - When they spend a Persona or Deed Artha, they can roll 1D6: on a 5 or 6, they do not lose that Artha and can spend it immediately again in the next session.

# Inspiration Concept

The Thao heritage draws inspiration from various Asian cultures, weaving together elements from different traditions to create a distinct and vibrant cultural tapestry. Their profound connection to the Mikado Empire, combined with their role as defenders and warriors, echoes the spirit of the samurai, emphasizing loyalty, honor, and adherence to a code of conduct. Through their rich history, unique physical traits, language, and the empowering "Blood of the Thao" quality, the Thao embody the essence of resilience, resourcefulness, and a deep reverence for their ancient roots.
