# History

In the annals of history, amid the tumultuous era of the First Crusade, an unassuming group emerged from the chaos—the nascent Peacekeepers. Originating as camp police, they were entrusted by the generals to maintain order among the rowdy soldiers. Their role extended beyond the battlefields; they were tasked with preventing internal conflicts and safeguarding the civilian population. A distinctive characteristic set them apart from the common soldiers—they were forbidden from donning armor, symbolizing a commitment to a different kind of warfare, one against lawlessness and disorder.

As the echoes of war gradually subsided and the Bell Lands began to take shape from the once-turbulent Lands of Earth and Water, the Peacekeepers underwent a profound transformation. The second Sedi Emperor, recognizing their unwavering commitment to maintaining peace in the burgeoning realm, elevated the Peacekeepers to the status of a knightly order. This pivotal moment in their history marked the formalization of their role as guardians of justice, and each Peacekeeper received a sword of justice in a solemn ceremony, bestowed upon them by the sitting Emperor.

The Sword of Justice became more than a symbolic token; it became a source of power and legitimacy for the Peacekeepers. In a land where law and order were paramount, the absence of this sword rendered a Peacekeeper as merely a common man. Legend has it that some daring criminals sought to evade the arms of justice by stealing the Sword of Justice itself. Without this emblem of authority, the judges were rendered powerless, and the perpetrators believed themselves beyond the reach of the law.

The authority of the Peacekeepers extended far and wide. Aside from the general of the imperial army, they held absolute authority to adjudicate laws and dispense justice. Their unique position in the legal hierarchy made them both respected and feared. In cases where the accused faced severe charges, and the Peacekeeper presiding over the matter felt that the evidence was not conclusive, a practice was established—the calling in of two additional Peacekeepers to form a quorum.

# Famous Cases In Peacekeeper History

## **The Trial of the Shadowed Assassin**

Peacekeeper Seraphina, known for her keen intellect and unyielding commitment to justice, found herself entangled in the enigma of a shadowed assassin. A series of mysterious murders had gripped the capital, with a clandestine figure leaving no trace but an enigmatic symbol at each crime scene. The accused, a hooded figure shrouded in darkness, was brought before Seraphina. The evidence was circumstantial, the whispers of the shadows revealing little. Faced with the gravity of the situation, Seraphina invoked the ancient right to form a quorum.

Two revered Peacekeepers, Eldric and Isolde, joined Seraphina to deliberate over the shadows that eluded the realm of evidence. The trial unfolded as a symphony of legal acumen, with each Peacekeeper contributing their insights. In the end, a unanimous decision was reached—the accused was acquitted due to insufficient evidence. While the public wanted the man hung, the Peacekeepers kept their honor and did not fall to the whims of the masses.

## **The Quest for the Stolen Sword**

The tranquility of the Peacekeepers was shattered when news arrived of a daring heist—a Sword of Justice had been stolen. Peacekeeper Margret, revered for her stoic demeanor and unassailable resolve, undertook the quest to retrieve the stolen symbol of authority. The thief, a cunning mastermind known as the Phantom Blade, had eluded capture for years, leaving behind a trail of stolen artifacts.

Margret, armed with her unwavering determination, even if she was removed from her rank ventured into the underbelly of the criminal underworld. Her journey took her through shadowed alleys and clandestine meeting places, unraveling the layers of conspiracy that shielded the Phantom Blade. As the pursuit intensified, Margret faced moral quandaries, questioning the very nature of justice and the extent to which she would go to reclaim the stolen Sword of Justice.

In the final confrontation, Margret stood face-to-face with the Phantom Blade. A tense exchange transpired, not only of blows but of ideals. The thief argued that the Peacekeepers, too, were bound by a sword, a symbolic representation of a power unchecked. Margret, resolute in her beliefs, ultimately subdued the Phantom Blade, retrieving the stolen sword and regaining her title. The incident left an indelible mark on the Peacekeepers, sparking internal debates about the symbol of their authority and the responsibilities it entailed.

## **The Legacy of the Silent Judge**

Peacekeeper Lucian, known as the Silent Judge, earned his epithet for his rare but profound interventions. In the case of the prodigious scholar accused of heresy, Lucian's wisdom and discernment were put to the test. The accused, a young Tested named Elara, stood accused of dabbling in Worm God magics and challenging the religious doctrines of the realm.

Lucian, with his silent contemplation, presided over the trial. The courtroom, usually filled with fervent arguments, witnessed a quiet and measured inquiry. Lucian's approach was unconventional, employing a Socratic method that led Elara to unravel her own beliefs and motivations. As the trial progressed, it became evident that Elara's pursuits were not driven by heresy but by an unquenchable thirst for knowledge.

In a rare moment of spoken wisdom, Lucian rendered his judgment—the accused was not guilty of heresy. The trial became a turning point, and Lucian's legacy endured as a reminder that justice was not only about wielding the Sword of Justice but also about understanding the complexities of the human spirit.

## Peacekeeper Training

Peacekeeper training is done at Chateau Euromi in the woods about 2 miles north of Oldwyn. To be nominated to be a Peacekeeper either they must have been a member of a Peacekeepers cadre for 10 years, or they are given a personal recommendation from the emperor himself.  Many noble families donate great gifts to the imperial house to get such a recommendation, but being allowed into the school does not guarantee entry into the ranks. While they will take in apprentices before the age of 15, they are not subjected to the trials until they are at least 16 years old.

New classes always start on the Coldest Night (the Winter Solstice), and training consists of 3 parts: the Arm, the Mind, and the Sword. Of the 3 tests, they are allowed to keep testing for 4 total years.  Some students pass all their tests in one year. Some take 4 years to complete. But those that can not pass in 4 years are barred from entry into the order.

### The Arm

Physical training is relentless. Under the watchful eyes of seasoned instructors, novices engage in grueling exercises that push the limits of their strength, agility, and stamina. Endurance runs through rugged terrains, weapon drills under the scorching sun, and combat simulations that mimic the unpredictability of real battles—all these are the crucibles that temper the bodies and spirits of the Peacekeeper hopefuls. One of the more unusual rituals of this test is they must refine the metal that will be used for their blade. Some students come with metal from their homelands or even swords of fallen Peacekeepers all to go into the crucible to make their own metal.

### The Mind

The intellectual aspect of training is equally demanding. Novices immerse themselves in the study of law, ethics, and the intricacies of the legal system they are sworn to protect. Being a Sword of Justice, they quickly learn, is not just a weapon—it is a symbol of their authority and the embodiment of the laws they uphold. Lessons on diplomacy, rhetoric, and the art of negotiation supplement their education, ensuring that they are not only warriors but also sagacious adjudicators.

### The Sword

The psychological resilience of Peacekeepers is cultivated through a series of challenges designed to expose them to the harsh realities they might face in their role. Simulated trials, where they must render judgments under pressure, are commonplace. These exercises not only refine their decision-making skills but also instill the gravity of their responsibilities. Novices learn to grapple with the complexities of morality, the shades of gray that permeate the pursuit of justice.

For those who graduate they are taken to the capital where they start a week of living in the elements, they give the Emperor their metal.  In the week the metalsmiths of the Emperor make their swords.  After that week is over there is a ceremonial bestowal of the Sword of Justice. In a sacred ceremony attended by the sitting Emperor, each novice is handed their sword, symbolizing their transition from mere trainees to Peacekeepers. The weight of the sword, both literal and symbolic, serves as a constant reminder of the responsibility that rests on their shoulders.  Their induction into the order is marked by a solemn oath to protect and serve, to be the unwavering guardians of justice in the realm. The Sword of Justice, now earned, becomes a revered companion that will accompany them on their journey, a constant reminder of the ideals they embody. While they are official Peacekeepers many then go and act as apprentices under other peacekeepers to learn even more for a year.

The Traditions and the Taboos of the Peacekeepers

- You can not wear armor more than padded, robes.
- You without your symbol of office, you can make no other rulings.
- You are allowed to demand room and board any home in the country for 1 night a year. After that, you must be formally invited to stay longer.

# Political Factions

While the Peacekeepers are one order, factions among them have grown with different views on how to keep the peace in the lands of the bell.

## **The Shepherds: Architects of Reform**

In the annals of Peacekeeper lore, the Shepherds stand as architects of reform, wielding compassion as their greatest weapon. Their origin story harks back to the aftermath of the First Crusade, a time when the Bell Lands were still healing from the scars of war. Among the ranks of the fledgling Peacekeepers emerged a group who envisioned a different approach—one that sought not only to dispense justice but also to shepherd the populace away from the path of criminality.

The founder of the Shepherds, Monica Gratsi, was a visionary known for her empathetic nature and a belief that not all crimes stemmed from irredeemable malevolence. She saw the underlying causes of criminal behavior—poverty, desperation, and a lack of opportunities—and sought to address these issues at their roots. Gathering like-minded individuals within the Peacekeeper order, Monica established the Shepherds, a faction dedicated to rehabilitation rather than retribution.

In the meeting Halls of the Order, Shepherds espouse understanding the socio-economic factors that contribute to crime, mastering negotiation techniques, and developing a keen awareness of mental health issues. This unique approach has earned the Shepherds both admiration and skepticism within the order.

In the field, Shepherds navigate the delicate balance between compassion and justice. Instead of rushing to trials and punitive measures, they engage with the community, seeking to address the root causes of criminality. Rehabilitation programs, vocational training, and community outreach are the hallmarks of their endeavors. Despite skepticism from some quarters, the Shepherds have witnessed remarkable success stories—individuals who, under their guidance, have transformed from lawbreakers to contributing members of society.

As the Shepherds continue to pioneer innovative approaches to criminal justice, their impact reverberates through the Bell Lands. Their belief in the potential for redemption challenges the conventional notions of punishment, sparking debates within the Peacekeeper order and beyond. Monica Gratsi's vision, conceived in the aftermath of war, has evolved into a compassionate movement that seeks not only to judge but to heal the wounds that breed criminality.

## **The Judges: Guardians of Legal Rigor**

In the labyrinthine corridors of Peacekeeper history, the Judges emerged as the steadfast guardians of legal rigor, demanding an active and unwavering adherence to the law. Their origin story intertwines with the earliest days of the Peacekeepers when the need for a disciplined and vigilant faction became apparent. The tumultuous era of the First Crusade laid the groundwork for the formation of the Judges, a faction that would uphold the law with an unyielding commitment.

At the helm of this faction was Lord Caius Braserbuck, a stern but principled leader who believed that a society without strict adherence to its laws was destined for chaos. Drawing inspiration from ancient legal traditions and a commitment to the rule of law, Braserbuck gathered individuals who shared his uncompromising vision. Together, they formed the Judges—a faction within the Peacekeepers tasked with ensuring that the laws of the Bell Lands were not only upheld but enforced with precision.

They are marked by an emphasis on legal scholarship, investigative techniques, and combat proficiency. Judges undergo intensive courses on the intricacies of Bell Lands law, historical legal precedents, and the philosophical underpinnings of justice. Physical training is equally rigorous, with an emphasis on martial skills to ensure that Judges can enforce the law in the face of resistance.

In the field, Judges serve as both adjudicators and enforcers. Their meticulous approach to investigations, adherence to legal protocols, and commitment to due process make them stalwart defenders of justice. Judges are often dispatched to handle complex legal cases, conduct thorough investigations, and ensure that every trial is a model of legal precision.

This faction, while respected for its unwavering commitment to the law, has not been without controversy. Critics argue that their strict interpretation of justice can lead to inflexibility and a lack of nuance in handling complex cases. Nevertheless, the Judges remain resolute, believing that the preservation of legal rigor is paramount for a just society.

## **The Executioners: Harbingers of Brutal Example**

In the darkened corners of Peacekeeper history, the Executioners loom as harbingers of brutal example, advocating that only through ruthless punishment can a country become law-abiding. Their origin story is entwined with the aftermath of the First Crusade, a time when the founders of the Peacekeepers grappled with the challenge of instilling a sense of fear and obedience in the wake of widespread lawlessness.

The central figure in the inception of the Executioners was Lord Alistair Whitestar, a stern and authoritarian leader who believed in the efficacy of harsh consequences. Witnessing the chaos that followed the First Crusade, Whitestar argued that a swift and brutal response to crime would serve as a deterrent, preventing the populace from straying into lawlessness. Thus, the Executioners were born—a faction within the Peacekeepers whose methods blurred the line between justice and vengeance.

The methods of Executioners diverge significantly from the other factions. While physical prowess and combat skills are paramount, the psychological conditioning focuses on desensitizing recruits to the harsh realities of their chosen path. Executioners undergo simulations of brutal punishments, witness the aftermath of violent crimes, and are taught to wield fear as a tool of control.

In the field, Executioners are often tasked with handling the most dangerous criminals—those deemed irredeemable by other factions. Their methods are controversial, involving public punishments, branding, and, in extreme cases, execution. The Executioners argue that the fear generated by their actions prevents others from straying into criminality, creating a society that is law-abiding through intimidation.

Critics within and outside the Peacekeeper order decry the methods of the Executioners as barbaric, arguing that justice should be tempered with mercy. However, proponents assert that in a world still healing from the wounds of war, brutal examples are necessary to prevent a descent into anarchy. The Executioners remain a divisive faction, their actions sparking debates on the ethical boundaries of justice within the realm.

# **The Hierarchy of the Peacekeepers: Guardians of Justice**

At the pinnacle of the Peacekeeper hierarchy stands the esteemed Prime Judge, a position of profound authority and responsibility. The ascension to this esteemed title is a testament to exceptional leadership and an unwavering commitment to the principles of justice. The Prime Judge holds a unique status within the order, either elected by a quorum of experienced Peacekeepers upon the demise of the previous Prime Judge or, in a scenario of imperial transition, the first Peacekeeper bestowed with the sword of justice by the new emperor assumes this esteemed role.

The Prime Judge bears the solemn duty of safeguarding the sanctity of the Peacekeepers. This extends to the right to block individuals from joining their esteemed ranks, a privilege rarely exercised but crucial to maintaining the order's integrity. Furthermore, the Prime Judge possesses the exclusive authority, second only to the emperor, to remove a standing Peacekeeper. This power is wielded judiciously, ensuring that those entrusted with upholding justice remain unwavering in their commitment.

Directly beneath the Prime Judge is the Master of the Euromi, a pivotal figure responsible for overseeing the rigorous training programs that mold recruits into formidable Peacekeepers. The Master of the Euromi plays a crucial role in selecting individuals deemed worthy to stand before the emperor and be anointed as Peacekeepers. This esteemed title is conferred by the Prime Judge, highlighting the interconnected nature of their roles in shaping the future guardians of justice.

The Master of the Euromi's domain includes the meticulous evaluation of potential recruits, assessing not only their physical prowess but also their ethical fortitude and unwavering dedication to justice. The responsibility of handpicking those who will bear the sword of justice underscores the critical role this title plays in the perpetuation of the Peacekeeper legacy.

Completing the triad of leadership is the Master of the Map, a title that brings forth an individual adept at strategic thinking and adept in the orchestration of Peacekeeper efforts across the vast expanse of the Bell Lands. This title is not bestowed lightly, and the one who assumes it must demonstrate a profound understanding of the intricacies of the realm. Appointed by the Prime Judge, the Master of the Map holds the authority to dispatch Peacekeepers to address critical issues and vital cases, playing a pivotal role in ensuring justice is upheld throughout the land.

Members chosen for specific missions are often granted a stipend, especially those who lack personal wealth. This ensures that the Peacekeepers, regardless of their financial standing, can fulfill their duty without impediment. The Master of the Map's strategic decisions and directives shape the course of Peacekeeper interventions, making them instrumental in the order's broader mission to maintain law and order across the diverse landscapes of the Bell Lands. The collaborative efforts of these three key figures form the bedrock of the Peacekeeper hierarchy, a bastion of justice that stands unwavering against the tides of chaos.

# Oath of the Peacekeepers

*In the presence of the Emperor and before the eyes of all who bear witness, I stand solemn and steadfast. With the Sword of Justice as my symbol and guide, I hereby swear:*

*To uphold the laws of the Bell Lands with unwavering integrity and impartiality. I shall embody justice, ensuring it is administered fairly and without prejudice, safeguarding the realm and challenge all those who will oppose it.  *

*To protect the innocent and defend the vulnerable. I shall be a shield for those who cannot defend themselves, and a light in the darkness, illuminating the path of justice.*

*To eschew armor and the trappings of war, committing myself to a different kind of battle—a battle against lawlessness and disorder. I shall rely on my wisdom, courage, and the Sword of Justice to maintain peace and order.*

*To stand resolute in the face of corruption and temptation. I shall resist any forces that seek to undermine justice, remaining steadfast in my duty, regardless of the personal cost.*

*To be a paragon of virtue and an exemplar of honor. I shall conduct myself with dignity and respect, upholding the noble traditions of the Peacekeepers and inspiring others by my actions.*

*To seek the truth and ensure that justice is not only done but seen to be done. I shall investigate thoroughly, judge fairly, and act decisively, mindful of the grave responsibilities I bear.*

*To remain loyal to the Emperor and the Imperial Throne. My allegiance is to the ideals of justice and the well-being of the realm, and I shall defend them with my life if necessary.*

*To honor the legacy of the Peacekeepers who have come before me and to leave a legacy of my own for those who will follow. I shall carry the torch of justice forward, ever vigilant and ever true.*

*By this oath, I bind myself to the sacred duty of a Peacekeeper. May my sword strike true, my heart remain pure, and my resolve be unbreakable. In the name of justice, I swear it.*

# Secrets of the Peacekeepers

## **Conflict of Shadow and Griffon:**

During the tumultuous Civil War between House Rivers and the reigning House Sedi, the Peacekeepers found themselves torn between loyalty and principle. Many among them sided with different factions, leading to internal conflicts where Peacekeeper fought against Peacekeeper. A unique challenge arose for those aligned with House Rivers, as they were unable to wield the symbolic swords given by the emperor due to their lack of allegiance to the sitting ruler. In response, a clandestine tradition emerged—the recruitment of non-Peacekeepers to fight for their convictions even when their masters were on the wrong side of history. This tradition has persisted through the ages, a reminder of the internal struggles faced by the Peacekeepers during times of political upheaval.

## **The Seal of the Throne:**

A profound secret lies within the sacred symbols of rulership. When the Bellringers ascended the Griffon Throne, the last of the Esten Kings bestowed upon them the Seal of the Throne—an obsidian seal intertwined with the roots of a mystical tree. Tradition dictates that each new emperor must touch this ancient seal during their oath of office. However, what remains unknown to the general populace is that this crucial artifact is not safeguarded by the Imperial House but is instead entrusted to the vigilant guardianship of the Peacekeepers of Oldwyn. This discreet arrangement, known only to a select few within the order, symbolizes the unique and intimate connection between the Peacekeepers and the imperial lineage—a bond that transcends the public eye.

## **The Faulty Census:**

Veiled in mystery, the Faulty Census has confounded the meticulous record-keeping of the Peacekeepers for generations. Despite the seemingly exhaustive efforts to account for every graduating student named Peacekeeper, a peculiar anomaly persists—an unexplained 5% discrepancy in the numbers. The secret lies in the existence of infiltrators, individuals who have cunningly integrated themselves into the order without detection. The enigma deepens as these infiltrators manage to evade the watchful eyes of their peers and mentors, despite the intimate knowledge each Peacekeeper possesses about their cohorts. Speculation runs rife among the ranks, with whispers of magical influences or covert alliances contributing to this Faulty Census, shrouding the true extent of the infiltration in secrecy. The pursuit of uncovering these hidden figures adds an element of suspense to the inner workings of the Peacekeepers, as they grapple with the elusive nature of those who walk among them as shadows.
