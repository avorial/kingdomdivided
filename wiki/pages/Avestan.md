# The Origin of the Avestan

In the primordial silence following the Calming of the Spheres, the worm gods had sedated Oh and broken his will, leaving the spheres connected only by ley lines. It was during this time that the Avestan, the first race to awaken, emerged from the soil of the heart. Unlike the old gods who walked among them, the Avestan could procreate and establish their lineage. Each Avestan possessed unique powers and abilities, and as they ranked themselves among the gods, they also ranked themselves among one another. This imbued them with a profound sense of order and hierarchy, mirroring the cosmic balance that had just been restored.

The Avestan were born into a strict societal structure where every individual had a defined place, bound by a chain of slavery and mastery. Each person was both a master to those below and a slave to those above, creating a vast network of interdependence. This intricate system allowed the flow of magical energies throughout the hierarchy, with power and resources trickling up and down the chain. At the apex of this pyramid-like structure sat the Masked Senate, a group of immortal rulers whose identities were hidden behind ancient masks. These masks symbolized continuity and control; if a senator was killed or contained, they would be replaced in secrecy, ensuring the illusion of eternal governance.

The Avestan were a people of striking bronze beauty, their appearance marked by regal features and a sense of timeless elegance. Their dark hair and piercing eyes reflected their inner strength and the deep-seated traditions of their race. As they awoke to the new world, they immediately set about establishing their dominance, their hierarchy lending them an unparalleled unity and purpose.

In the early days of their civilization, the Avestan encountered the Thrani, another ancient race that had survived the cosmic upheavals. The Thrani, led by the Patient One, were beings of golden skin and deep wisdom. Recognizing the potential for mutual benefit, the Avestan formed an alliance with the Thrani. The Avestan provided structure and order, while the Thrani contributed their vast knowledge of ancient magics.

With this unity, they birthed the Kadal Empire. Named after the endless void the spheres float in, the Kadal Empire would be as limitless as the heavens. With the bonds of magic, they enslaved the world, becoming its masters. With the old gods as their allies, there was no land they did not own. Until the time of Bano.

Bano believed that there was one entity not yet enslaved or mastered: Oh himself. Using a group of beasts and monsters, Bano traveled the caves and veins of Oh, fighting past any who tried to stop his ambition. After a decade, he arrived at the still heart of Oh, weakened by the devouring of the worm gods. Using forbidden magics of the stars and the worms, he awoke Oh’s heart to give consent to submit to Bano as his master.

This fool's errand awoke the heart, causing it to start beating again, shaking the sphere and destroying the land, breaking it like a plate hitting the ground. Bano’s people fled the sphere as it rumbled, sparing them from the destruction that shattered the unity of Kadal. Millions died, and the empire was forced to retreat. With one-third of their old domains left, the Avestan became rare outside the remaining lands of Kadal, protected by thousands of master and slave bonds.

Realizing that a single emperor could not manage the aftermath of the heartbeat disaster, the Avestan convened a council to name new leaders. These became the Masked Senate, the ultimate authority, their immortality secured through ancient rituals and the unbroken succession of masked replacements. These rulers wielded immense power, drawing upon the collective energies of their people. The masks they wore were not just symbols but powerful artifacts that connected them to the lifeblood of Avestan society.

Within the smaller borders of Kadal, the Avestan’s mastery of their strict hierarchical system allowed them to achieve great feats of magic and construction. They built towering cities, created complex networks of trade and communication, and developed powerful enchantments that bolstered their dominion. The magical pyramid scheme of energy flow made the Avestan formidable, as those at the top of the chain could harness the collective power of those below them.

The Avestan’s societal structure was not without its challenges. Ambition and power struggles were common, as individuals sought to climb the hierarchy and gain more control. However, the unyielding framework established by the Masked Senate ensured that these conflicts rarely threatened the overall stability of their society.

Together, the Avestan and Thrani forged a civilization that blended the strengths of both races. The Avestan’s hierarchical mastery and the Thrani’s magical prowess created a powerful and harmonious society. The Masked Senate and the leaders of the Thrani worked together to guide their people, ensuring the prosperity and stability of their shared world.

With their failed empire broken, the Avestan rarely crossed the seas to nearby countries. They presumed that the descendants of their former slaves had built great empires, ready to enact vengeance for the oppression caused by the Kadal. This isolationism allowed them to invest deeply in their own domains, where the bonds of slavery and mastery were seen not as chains but as the threads that wove their society together, creating a tapestry of strength, unity, and purpose. Through their unyielding adherence to this system, the Avestan ensured their place as the dominant force in their lands, a falcon in a golden cage.
