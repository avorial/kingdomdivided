# Events

Ten years before the events of the game, Siobhán Silverridge is a child living with her father, Kincaid Silverridge, and mother, Ursula Henge-Silverridge. The family is summering in Deerholt, which is used as country escape for the noble families of Silverridge, Reinhardt, and McBravo. Siobhán wakes for the morning, slowly waking up as she chats with the serving maid, Agnes, and the serving girl, Emma. As Ursula rises, she urges Siobhán to make ready for her picnic with her aunt, Hailey Silverridge-Reinhardt, reminding her to not smudge her clothes. She is very distant and careful in dealing with her daughter, not making physical contact.

As Siobhán eats and wakes up, she cuddles with her father as he and her mother discuss some situation back in Windbreak that seems to be cutting off their trip to Deerholt early. Ursula’s father, Lord Lucas Henge, seems to be in some sort of conflict with Duke Malcolm, but Siobhán, as a child, doesn’t pay much attention to this detail. She dresses at her mother’s instruction and prepares for her day with her aunt.

Siobhán and Emma, the serving girl, go to find her aunt, Hailey, who is in the open courtyard with her husband, Karl Reinhardt, and their young son, Hans. Karl and Hailey are very affectionate with one another, and it is clear they are very much in love. Karl and Kincaid have made plans for a ride, taking Hans with them, strapped to Karl. Siobhán makes a show of her manners, curtseying to her aunt and presenting the strawberries she brought for a gift. It is clear that Hailey was the one that had a hand in instructing Siobhán as a child in manners and grace, and that Siobhan had been very close to Hailey. Once the niceties are completed, Siobhán rushes to hug her aunt, openly affectionate with her in a way she is not with her more distant warrior mother. As she touches her, she feels a strange, electric tingle feeling, but being a child doesn’t understand it or really take note of it.

With Emma in tow, carrying their supplies, the three of them track out of the village to find a special glade where Hailey has planned their picnic. Hailey discusses some of the Silverridge family history, sharing a story of Emir Ramirez, who was a cunning warrior, but very short, which makes Siobhán giggle, asking how he could be so small and a great warrior. Hailey explains that Emir couldn’t conquer the lands or be so great on his own, that he had the help of his Ramirez brothers, (who form House Ramirez) as well as House Wycliffe, and his network of spies who helped him unite the nations.

Hailey explains that not only was she the oldest, but that she was 13-years-older than Connor, the next oldest. She was charming and spirited, and Malcolm’s favorite. He could be a father for her in a way that he never was for Connor, Rory, Sharon, and Kincaid (noting their mother, Naomi, was bed-ridden for a year after Kincaid’s birth). She lectures how each of them performed a role in the family. Rory is like Emir’s brothers, the one who is loyal and helps get the job done for Connor, the heir, while Kincaid married into the Henge family. They were not friendly with the Silverridges until Kincaid married Ursula, making them friends, highlighting the importance of marriage. Siobhán brings up her grandfather, Lucas Henge, and his issues with Malcolm, and Hailey explains that they are arguing over sharing toys - Lucas wants more sharing of power with Malcolm.

Hailey clearly filled the role among the siblings that Siobhán does among the cousins, the charming one who smooths things over with the nobles, who uses her graces and manners and intelligence to pave the way with them and smooth over Malcolm’s rougher edges. Her best friend with the then Duchesse of Montimier (formerly a McBravo), who was in love with Duke Charles Montimier. Malcom had intended Hailey to marry Duke Charles, but she fell in love with Karl Reinhardt, so arranged to marry him instead and made Malcom accept it. Hailey urges Siobhán to remember that the key to success is to always have a lot of friends and people she knows will support her family, and gives Siobhán more lessons on how to make people like her.

Once they arrive in the lovely glade, they immediately see a singular white tree standing there. Siobhan makes note of it and rushes up to it, and it turns out that it is magical, bowing to Siobhán. The closer she gets, the more anthropomorphic it becomes. It is even more noticeable the closer that Hailey comes towards it. Hailey explains it is a magic tree, which they cannot touch, because it could potentially do harm to the tree. Siobhán notices, and urges Emma not to touch the tree, though she wants to. Hailey states there is a legend about the magic of the tree and why the Estin’s hair - and by extension, Lyla’s - is white.

They have their lunch, but as they do, the weather begins to turn in a strange and magical way, which Siobhán doesn’t notice, but Hailey does. She has the girls help her pack up and rushes them through the first, even as the girls are slow and reluctant. As they come out of the forest  and finally spot the Bellringer tower, they make their way on the road, they find their path cut off by strange warriors - the Kanya.

The Kanya say they are there to take something from Hailey. Hailey plans herself and says they won’t take it from her. Hailey replies to them, saying something in Salt-Blood, a language Siobhán doesn’t understand. They laugh at her. In the distance, Siobhán sees a group of riders led by her father, Kincaid, and her uncle, Karl. As they ride towards them, however, the Kanya pull a smoke rope that appears and trips the horses, sending the men flying off. Seeing this, Hailey prepared to fight. She plants her feet and prepares to fight, using the basket as a weapon, as Siobhán and Emma run for the forest.

The girls run into the trees and don’t get lost, managing to climb up a tree, high enough to not be seen and to have a view of the village burning.  It is there they see the Kanya are there, shooting and injuring villagers, but outright killing the nobles they find. Siobhán catches sight of Hailey in the distance and notices there is a sort of aura around her.

Just below them, at the base of the tree, a group of Kanya gather, discussing how they are looking for two specific targets, Hailey and Ursula, who have apparently stolen something from the Kanye, but they don’t explain what. It clicks that Emir’s spy master, the one Hailey mentioned, was likely a Kanya, and they feel that Emir betrayed them and stole something from them and they want something back.

By this point, Ursula has joined the fight, and Kincaid and Karl have rejoined the fight. It is clear that Ursula has the same sort of aura that Hailey has. They are fighting furiously, and defeat the Kanya there. While they could use the opportunity to escape, Ursula refuses to, clearly looking for Siobhán. Meanwhile, Karl stops to take Han’s off his front, where he had be strapped, and restart him on his back, but in the middle of that the Kanya teleport back in a cloud of smoke and stab Karl with a knife. Karl takes the knife and throws it back at his attacker, killing him.

Meanwhile, Hailey has been holding her own with her attackers, but they finally overpower her, much to Siobhán’s horror. They knock her to her knees, and slit her throat, cutting her head off. The aura that had surrounded Hailey disappears. Her blood spills and pools on the ground, where it begins to evaporate and disappear. The evaporation seems to throw the Kanya into an ecstatic stupor, paralyzing them.

Ursula sees their stupor and decides to use this as her chance to cut down the Kanya, as Karl and Kincaid throw themselves in the fray. However, Ursula becomes surrounded. It is clear they are about to attack and kill her. Having just witnessed her beloved aunt horrifically murdered, and seeing her own mother about to have the same, Siobhán screams in anguish for her mother. In that moment, the aura that had been surrounding Ursula now leaves her, moving to Siobhán. She now sees the world in ultra color and definition, like she never had before. Instead of killing Ursula, they pin her hand to the ground with a blade, and turn their attention on Siobhán.

She jumps from the tree, as Emma flees to the next tree. Siobhán runs to her mother. They are surrounded by the Kanya, who turn their crossbows on them both. Ursula, who has freed her injured hand, grabs Siobhán and turns her away, shielding her from the crossbow bolts which lodge themselves in Ursula instead. Siobhán can feel the vibration of the bolts through her mother’s body. Suddenly, Kincaid and Karl ride up on horses, scattering the Kanya, and helping Ursula and Siobhán up onto a horse. They ride from the village, and as they do, the Bellringer Tower that had stood so high over the village, explodes in fire.

At the crossroads where years later, Siobhán and her cousins would first meet Louis Covinguard, the group comes across Malcolm and Connor, along with Lucas Henge, riding from Windbreak, already fully armored and ready to fight. Malcom and Lucas are clearly already aware of the Kanya attack on the village, (Connor is not, which is why he doesn’t remember this in the future). Because the adults are so injured, they have to stop and help the injured rather than ride to the village and stop the Kanya. They get a local monk, who Siobhan now realizes was O'rih, to help heal all the wounded. Siobhán, who is in a traumatized stupor after everything, doesn’t really remember any of this until this memory breaks through to her in her sleep in the future, as she realizes she has had a vision of her past.
