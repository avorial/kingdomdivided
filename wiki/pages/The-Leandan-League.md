**The Government of Leandan, a brief overview **

- The **Capitol **is the **City of Senex**, found at the end of the Senex Sound in Katome (the city at the end of the long inlet running between Ida, Kythnos, Lugripia, Altinum, and Katome)
- The **number Three (3)  **is highly revered by all Leandan. It’s how many Acts of Community Kato Leandan gave them, it’s how many ships he came with, it’s how he said they should govern themselves. A three-legged stool is more stable on uneven surfaces than a four-legged stool. There can be no tie with three. *It is a plurality of representation to Bellkind’s singularity of authority.*
- There are **Three Chancellors**, elected by all Major Guild Masters, who run the League as a whole; no GM, former or current, can stand as Chancellor. A term is **12 years** and you can run for **2 terms**. They do not need to be consecutive.
- **Five Guildmasters** act as **Small Council** to the Chancellors - 3 Major GMs, 2 Minor GMs. They have** 6-year terms** and can run for **2 terms**. They are staggered terms so there is only one election at a time per seat. The **Chancellors select the 5 Privy Council Members **with the approval of the Major Guild Masters. One chair is up for re-election every 3 years. - While an individual Guildmaster may not run for more than 2 terms, the Guild itself can certainly be reelected consecutively. Chaining the retirement of one to the appointment of another Guildmaster, so as to maintain a Guild’s place on the Small Council, is not unheard of.
- A Province is run by the **triumvirate **of **one Governor, one Marshal, and one local Guild Master** (major or minor; only a representative, not a GM)
- A **Marshal **governs the** military force** on behalf of the Governor/Province. That force is a **local militia** built from men and women of the area. It is inspired by the New Model Army of England (The Roundheads, [*https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Model_Army*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Model_Army))
- A **Guild**, whether Major or Minor, is run by **1 Guild Master**, and then her **Left and Right Hands**, for a total of **three individuals**. Each Hand has its distinct duties.

**Starts with an End:௫ and the People of Leandan:**

[*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/lore/legend_of_oh*](https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/lore/legend_of_oh)

In the Leandan ethos, deeply rooted in the teachings of Kato Leandan, lies the belief that the next incarnation of O will erupt from their nation, spilling all and one into the sea. Embracing this prophecy while dismissing the ‘gods’ as panderful nuisances, Kato Leandan imparted the qualities that would help his people stand independent, stand apart from O. As Leandans strive to create a society of abundance and organization, they recognize that only from such fertile grounds can the next incarnation of O possibly emerge. To maintain their individuality and honor their beliefs, Leandans practice cremation, viewing it as a symbolic return to the abundant earth. Whispers persist of secret burials among some, like the Welvert, who cling to their Kadalian traditions in the face of Leadan evolution.

**The Three Acts of Community, as taught by Kato Leandan**

1. Act of Public Service: "You can only succeed as much as your neighbor can."

This act is a cornerstone of the community's ethos, emphasizing the interconnectedness of every individual within the League. In the Act of Public Service, members recognize that their success is intimately tied to the prosperity of their neighbors. It inspires a commitment to actively contribute to the well-being of the entire community. Through acts of public service, whether it's lending a helping hand in times of need, volunteering expertise, or participating in communal projects, individuals ensure that the collective success of the League is prioritized over individual achievements. This act fosters a culture of shared responsibility, where the strength of the community is built upon the support and service provided by each member.

2. Act of Unity: "We stand together or we stand not at all."

At the heart of community strength lies the Act of Unity, encapsulating the idea that true resilience comes from standing united. This act encourages members to put aside differences and work towards common goals. It promotes collaboration, mutual respect, and the understanding that a unified front is essential for facing challenges and pursuing shared aspirations. The Act of Unity inspires individuals to look beyond individual interests and embrace a collective identity. By standing together, the League becomes a formidable force, capable of weathering any storm and achieving greater heights than individuals acting in isolation. This act reinforces the notion that unity is the bedrock upon which the community's success is built.

3. Act of Legacy: "Carry what we learn into the future to grow from."

The Act of Legacy encapsulates the commitment to preserving the wisdom and values that have guided the League to success. It emphasizes the importance of passing down the collective knowledge, experiences, and principles to future generations. Through education, storytelling, and intentional efforts to document the community's journey, the Act of Legacy ensures that the League's history is not forgotten. Instead, it becomes a source of inspiration and guidance for those who come after. This act fosters a sense of continuity and purpose, encouraging individuals to learn from the past, adapt to changing circumstances, and contribute to the ongoing growth and evolution of the League. It reinforces the idea that the community's strength lies not just in its present achievements but in its enduring legacy.

**The Provinces of Leadan**

**Katome (KAH-tohme)**

Katoites - Blonde Nordic

[[Katoites|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/katoites*]]

Minor Cultures: Welvart

Nestled within the embrace of verdant landscapes, the Province of Katome stands as a testament to the enduring legacy of Kato Leandan, a visionary who championed the ideals of public service as a moral imperative. Named in his honor, the city thrives on the principles of community, cooperation, and stewardship. The fertile land surrounding Katome provides a rich tapestry for both farming and forestry, embodying the essence of sustainability that Leandan so fervently advocated. Lush fields yield bountiful harvests, sustaining a populace whose ethos is rooted in communal well-being. The rhythmic hum of both agriculture and forestry intertwines, creating a symbiotic relationship with the land that Kato Leandan once walked, leaving an indelible mark on the city that became a capital: Senex. As the sun sets over the fields and forests of Katome, the echoes of a visionary's call to public service continue to resonate through the collective spirit of its people.

It is home to the Major Guilds of Harmony, Physicians, and Scribes, and the Minor Guilds of Arbiters, Apothecaries, (Assassins), Bookbinders, Storytellers, and Sculptors.

**Ida (EYE-dah)**

Welvart (Velvart) - Dutch, Can Speak Any Language

[[Welvart|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/welvart*]]

Minor Cultures: Katoites

The Province of Ida, a flourishing realm named in homage to Ida Leandan, the illustrious daughter of the visionary founder Kato Leandan, bears witness to a legacy of unyielding character and perceptive wisdom. Nestled in a landscape teeming with abundance, Ida's province thrives on the harmonious coexistence of farming, forestry, and the lucrative pursuit of crystal mining. Known far and wide for its industrious spirit, Ida has become a beacon of prosperity within the country. The province's bustling markets echo the lively exchange of goods, particularly renowned for its crystal mines and forestry that contribute to the region's economic vitality. Skilled artisans such as woodworkers, carpenters, cartwrights, and architects lend their expertise, shaping the province into a hub of creativity and craftsmanship. In the Province of Ida, the synergy of tradition and innovation paints a vibrant tapestry, reflecting the enduring legacy of a remarkable lineage and the industrious spirit that defines this flourishing realm.

It is home to the Major Guilds of Weaponsmiths and Woodworkers, and the Minor Guilds of Architect, Fletching, Carpenter, Cartwrights, Foresters, and Logging.

**Umbria (uhm-BREE-uh)**

Welvart (Velvart) - Dutch, Can Speak Any Language

[[Welvart|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/welvart*]]

Minor Cultures: Katoites, Quintill

As the third province established within the esteemed Leandan League, the Province of Umbria stands as a bastion of metallurgical excellence and mineral abundance. Renowned throughout the Capitol as the premier armorer, Umbria has become synonymous with craftsmanship that transcends the ordinary. The province's mastery in mining, especially the coveted Fire Crystal, positions it at the forefront of innovative design in armor and weaponsmithing. The hum of industry resonates through the mountains, echoing the dedication of skilled artisans who transform raw metals into works of both functional prowess and aesthetic beauty. Along the picturesque coast, the resounding presence of the Whaler's Guild makes itself known, becoming a vital waypoint for seafarers and adventurers. The fine ships of Ostia find their first port of call in Umbria, a testament to the province's strategic significance and the enduring legacy of the Leandan League's visionary pursuits.

It is home to the Major Guilds of Armorsmiths, Miners, and Weaponsmiths, as well as the Minor Guilds of Artillery, Automaton, Machinist, Whalers, and Bladesmiths.

**Morini (more-REE-NEE)**

Katoites - Blonde Nordic

[[Katoites|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/katoites*]]

Minor Cultures: Paltrists

Nestled within the gently rolling hills and bordered by the serene expanse of both ocean and Lake Näsi, the Province of Morini stands as a testament to the delicate balance between nature and civilization. Here, it is said that the land belongs more to the animals than it does to the people, as Morini is renowned for hosting some of the realm's finest animal husbandries. The inhabitants of Morini have cultivated a deep connection with the fauna that grace its vast meadows, weaving a harmonious tapestry of coexistence. In this province, cooking is not merely sustenance but a refined art form, with culinary traditions as diverse as the landscapes that stretch across the horizon. Moreover, Morini proudly boasts itself as the birthplace of Veterinarian medicine, where skilled practitioners have honed their craft in the service of both domestic and wild creatures. As the sun sets over the tranquil waters of Lake Näsi, Morini whispers tales of a symbiotic relationship between its people, the animals, and the land they all call home.

It is home to the Major Guilds of Culinarians and Farmers and the Minor Guilds of Bakers, Butchers, Husbandry, Veterinarians, and Vintners.

**Altinum (al’TIN-num)**

Paltrist - Spanish and North African

[[Paltris|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/paltris*]]

Minor Cultures: Katoites, Thrani, Welvart

Curving gracefully into the lush, tropical landscape, the Province of Altinum enchants with its natural splendor and captivating sunsets that drape the sky in hues of gold and crimson. Embraced by the expansive ocean, the tranquil shores of Lake Näsi, and the lively Senex Sound, Altinum reveals itself as a realm where meticulous craftsmanship blends seamlessly with the gentle, unhurried pull of the afternoon. Recognized for its pristine beaches adorned with fine white, sparkling sand, Altinum has perfected the art of transforming this natural treasure into the realm's most exquisite glass products. The rhythmic waves that caress the shore seem to share the secrets of skilled artisans who delicately craft glassware, reflecting the beauty that envelops them. As the sun gracefully descends below the horizon, casting a warm glow upon the tranquil waters, Altinum stands as a testament to the quiet strength found in the harmonious synthesis of natural wonders and the craftsmanship that elevates them to unparalleled heights.

It is home to the Major Guild of the Glasssmiths, as well as the Minor Guilds of Glassware, and Lensers.

**Ostia (OH-stee-ah)**

Quintill - Northern France (Sun King)

[[Quintill|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/quintill*]]

Minor Cultures: Zafroff

Nestled as a maritime guardian between the distant Holy Land of Figor and the mainland, the island Province of Ostia plays a pivotal role as both a barrier and a bridge against the Holy Land's fervent religious factions. Stretching along its length is a central mountain ridge, carving the landscape into a dramatic symphony of peaks and valleys. Ports and fjords dot its coasts, creating a haven for maritime enthusiasts and earning Ostia the distinction of being the finest home for the revered Shipwrights Guild. In this island realm, sailing, fishing, and whaling reign supreme dominating popular culture and weaving through everyday conversations. Ostia stands as a testament to the delicate balance between serving as a trade nexus and nurturing its own seafaring traditions, all against the breathtaking backdrop of its rugged, mountainous terrain.

It is home to the Major Guild of the Shipwrights and the Minor Guilds of Cartographers, Navigators, Netmakers, and Sailmakers.

**Lugripia (lu-GRIP-pee-ah)**

Paltrist - Spanish and North African

[[Paltris|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/paltris*]]

Minor Cultures: Katoites, Thrani, Welvart

The Province of Lugripia, adorned with a tapestry of vibrant wildflowers and boasting a diverse array of rare foliage and trees, stands as a testament to nature's beauty and bounty. However, beyond its natural allure, Lugripia holds a distinguished reputation as the fashion capital of the League. From the abundance of wildflowers and rare foliage, artisans and designers draw inspiration to create exquisite garments and accessories that set trends throughout the realm. Additionally, Lugripia is endowed with substantial deposits of metal, which it generously supplies to other provinces for the crafting of armor, weapons, and jewelry, further cementing its pivotal role in the League's economy. Sharing the majestic Mt Inara with its neighboring province of Altinum, Lugripia stands as a symbol of both natural splendor and artistic innovation within the League.

It is home to the Major Guild of the Tailors and the Minor Guilds of Dyers, Masons, and Stonecutters.

**Kythnos (KYTH-noes)**

Katoites - Blonde Nordic

[[Katoites|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/katoites*]]

Minor Cultures: Paltrist, Quintill

Ever ready at the mouth of the Senex Sound before it opens into the vast ocean, the Province of Kythnos unfolds in a mesmerizing landscape. Rolling hills gracefully transition into a formidable mountain range, offering an array of farmland meticulously utilized in the province's flourishing fiber arts. Kythnos is not only celebrated for the diversity of its agricultural practices but also for being a haven of rare green healing crystals, carefully mined and treasured for their unique properties. The exceptional beauty of Kythnos is not confined to its landscapes alone; its citizens, known for their peculiar yet captivating charm, add an extra layer of allure to the province's character. Bordered by the Senex Sound, the expansive ocean, and the serene shores of Lake Balaton, Kythnos stands as a sanctuary where the interplay of natural resources and the extraordinary beauty of its people create a harmonious tapestry.

It is home to the Major Guild of Parnassian and Weavers, and the Minor Guilds of Actors, Distillers, Painting, and Undertakers.

**Corai** **(KOR-eye)** (Protectorate of)

Thrani - South Asian

[[Thrani|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/thrani*]]

Minor Cultures: Thao, Welvart

Standing as a steadfast bulwark to the Mikado Empire, the island Protectorate of Corai forms one half of an exotic duo, its fate intertwined with its island-sister, Corumia. Despite a history marked by turbulence, the two find peace in the shared port of the harmonious Brotherhood City. Corai, famed for its bountiful fishing grounds, boasts an even greater renown for its exceptional beers. Long-standing whispers suggest that Corai is the home of the League's most unique yeasts and fermentation agents, a secret jealously guarded by the island. At the heart of this brewing mastery lies the Grand Guild of Brewers, an esteemed institution that solidifies Corai's reputation as a haven for those who appreciate the fine art of brewing. In the face of regional complexities, Corai stands not just as a protectorate but as a connoisseur's paradise where the ocean's bounty and the alchemy of brewing converge in perfect harmony.

It is home to the Major Guild of Brewers and the Minor Guild of Boffins.

**Corumia (cor-ROOM-me-ah)** (Protectorate of)

Thao - Chinese/Japanese/Korean (Mikado)

[[Thao|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/thao*]]

Minor Cultures: Helior, Thrani, Welvart

Once part of the Mikado Empire, the island Protectorate of Corumia, along with its counterpart Corai, undertook a careful negotiation for an exit, eventually finding refuge within the mindful embrace of the Leandan League. Despite their shared history under the Mikado, Corumia now stands as a proud bridge between the two nations. Home to a remarkable colony of giant Helior, it serves not only as a symbol of resilience but also as a pivotal point of resistance. Corumia strategically positions itself as a bridge for trade with the distant territory of Maj, fostering a steady flow of exotic goods into the League's hands. The island's skilled workforce specializes in leather and leatherwork, creating a thriving industry that reflects its resilience and determination. Furthermore, Corumia proudly houses the largest concentration of Gray Crystals, the essential 'batteries' that power the ingenuity of the Automaton Guild, solidifying its role as a vital contributor to the League's technological advancements.

It is home to the Major Guild of Leatherworkers and the Minor Guilds of Fishermen and Midwifery.

**Frisii (fur’E-see)**

Quintill - Northern France (Sun King)

[[Quintill|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/quintill*]]

Minor Cultures: Katoites, Saltblood

Basking in opulence, the Province of Frisii stands as the crown jewel of wealth within the expanse of Leandan. Its affluence is woven into the very fabric of the land, primarily stemming from an abundance of precious crystals – Gold, Fire, and even the rare Obsidian. These gems find their homes within the subterranean galleries of Frisii, giving the province an unmatched allure. At the heart of this wealth lies the extraordinarily powerful Jewelers Guild, a collective of skilled artisans who harness the magic within these crystals to create unparalleled masterpieces. The province gracefully crescents around the serene shores of Lake Balaton, with its back warmed by the embrace of the ocean. Once found within the borders of Frisii, the City-state of Cataclistos was the crown of the province before gaining its emancipation and achieving independence. This transformative event resulted in a fission between Cataclistos and Frisii, a divergence that, despite Frisii's efforts to downplay, remains an indelible mark on their shared history. Nonetheless, this emancipation stands as a testament to the historical significance of both entities and the enduring legacy of prosperity that defines Frisii's character.

It is home to the Major Guilds of Jewelers and Smiths and the Minor Guilds of Finesmith, Gemist, Gem Cutter, Locksmith, and Sculpting.

**Cataclistos (cat-ta-CLEES-s’tos)** (City-state of)

Quintill - Northern France (Sun King)

[[Quintill|*https://kdwiki.avorial.com/en/ancestry/quintill*]]

The independent City-state of Cataclistos, once an integral part of the province of Frisii, underwent a transformative shift some 400 years ago upon the discovery of the astonishing blue Sky Crystals. Sky Crystals are what allow the Sky Trade whatsoever. Understood to be the sole known location in all of Leandan harboring these coveted gems, Cataclistos guards them fiercely under the vigilant protection of the Airship Guild. With a keen belief that these crystals hold the key to their autonomy, the Airship Guild in Cataclistos aspires to liberate themselves from the dominion of the Shipwright's Guild. Independence and personal responsibility stand as cultural touchstones for Cataclistos, shaping the ethos of a city-state that has boldly carved its identity amidst the iridescent blue glow of its Sky Crystals.

Cataclistos was originally the city of *Narbonne *before its independence, wherein it renamed itself after Kato Leandan’s first ship.

It houses no Major Guild Hall found here. However, it is home to the independently spirited Minor Guild, the Airship Guild.
