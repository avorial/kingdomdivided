### **House Vathi (formerly House Thaphicarea)**

![house_vathi](/wiki/media/house_vathi.png)

**Symbol:** A blazing phoenix rising from flames  
**Words:** "From Ashes, We Feast"

House Vathi, formerly known as House Thaphicarea, has always been synonymous with fire and passion. Their legacy dates back to the era of the Esten Kings, a time when their mastery over flame and fervor set them apart as vital houses in the realm. Thaphicarea’s dominion was marked by their vibrant and fiery culture, commonly known for their quick actions that were not always well thought out. Other Esten Kings use to say, "no fire burns so bright that is not fed by the wood of the esten".

#### **History and Transformation**

During the invasion of Ethan Sedi, House Thaphicarea chose a different path from many of their contemporaries. Recognizing the impending doom that would befall them should they remain, they fled to the distant Mikado Empire with the Lulumia. This exodus allowed them to preserve their lineage and traditions, albeit in a foreign land. The remnants who stayed over time, they re-established themselves as House Vathi in the Bell Lands, a name derived from the old Esten word for "ash."

The Thaphicarea in the Mikado Empire found themselves in a land with rich traditions of its own, blending their fiery heritage with the new culture they encountered. This fusion led to a unique amalgamation of customs and practices, with over time sending these traditions to their brothers of House Vathi.

#### **Culture and Characteristics**

House Vathi is renowned for its intense and impassioned nature. Their members are often characterized by their fiery temperaments and unwavering determination. They are known to excel in fields that require a blend of creativity and fervor, such as arts, military strategy, and alchemy. The Vathi people are also skilled pyromancers, their control over fire seen as both a gift and a mark of their noble heritage.

**Traditions:**

- **The Phoenix Festival:** An annual celebration marking the house's rebirth and resilience. The festival involves elaborate fire dances, storytelling of their ancestors' bravery, and symbolic acts of setting aflame and renewing key elements of their culture.
- **Forge of Passions:** If a youngling is shown as marked by the flame, they then prove their mastery over fire and their inner passions by forging a weapon or artifact that symbolizes their personal journey and the house's enduring spirit.
- **Ash Eating: **Any foe that vexes a member of house Vathi for more than a year, when bested is burned to ash and eaten at festive meal. It is seen as a special blessing of the old gods to be able to do this ritual.

#### **Current Standing**

Despite their diminished status compared to the days of the Esten Kings, House Vathi holds a respected position in the Bell Lands. Their expertise in pyromancy and their vibrant culture make them influential in various domains, particularly in arts and warfare. The house maintains a network of alliances, both with their brothers in the Mikado and with local powers in the Bell Lands, ensuring their continued survival and relevance.

In summary, House Vathi stands as a testament to resilience and transformation. From the ashes of Thaphicarea, they have risen, carrying forward the flame of their ancestors' legacy and igniting new paths of glory in foreign lands.
