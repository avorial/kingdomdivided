House Menes, a profoundly unique house in the Almirn Duchy, stands at the crossroads of diverse cultures. Situated merely two days from their capital are the borders of three other duchies, creating an intricate web of connections and potential conflicts. In the delicate dance of diplomacy, House Menes endeavors to mend fences with their neighboring regions, understanding the fragile nature of their position. However, they are keenly aware that this harmony is temporary, merely postponing the inevitable clashes that loom on the horizon. Across the border, Montimer troops raid Nyiborog, Esten extremists wreak havoc in Felixstowe, and Pearstone engages in conflicts with Pelists cults south of the river.

Navigating the intricate web of regional politics, House Menes strives to keep Almerin interests at the forefront while treading carefully to avoid provoking their neighbors excessively. This delicate balancing act has been a formidable and ongoing challenge for House Menes, yet they have, for the most part, succeeded. Through strategic marriages and diplomatic finesse, they've managed to secure victories without escalating tensions to a breaking point. However, the members of House Menes are pragmatic; they understand that fate may eventually catch up to them. In preparation for an uncertain future, they've initiated a strategy of sending their children to far-flung corners of the country. This move serves the dual purpose of creating valuable connections and ensuring that, when the inevitable war arrives, House Menes will have allies and influence scattered across the realms. The weaving of this cultural tapestry by House Menes is not only a survival strategy but a testament to their resilience and adaptability in the face of an ever-changing political landscape.
