# History

The Helior race traces its lineage back to the ancient giants of old, known for their immense size and strength. However, through years of interbreeding with humans, they underwent a transformation, becoming a distinct humanoid race known as the Helior. This process gradually reduced their size compared to their giant ancestors, but they still retain a formidable stature, with an average height ranging from 6'5" to 7'5". Despite their diminished size, their inherent physical prowess and heritage as descendants of giants remain deeply ingrained within them. The Helior carry the legacy of their ancestors, embodying their strength, resilience, and an undercurrent of deep-seated rage.

# Stereotypes

The Helior are often revered as the epitome of physical power and fortitude. They are renowned for their exceptional strength, towering presence, and unwavering bravery. Others perceive them as natural leaders and formidable warriors, viewing their martial prowess as unmatched. However, these stereotypes sometimes overshadow their individuality and diverse talents, as they possess a range of skills beyond the battlefield. It is essential to note that to harness and control their deep-seated rage, the Helior structure their lives within extreme structures and orders. Without a purpose to serve, a Helior may quickly succumb to mindless acts of violence.

# Biological Traits

The Helior retain certain biological traits reminiscent of their giant ancestors. While their size is reduced compared to the ancient giants, they possess a larger and more muscular build than humans. This stature grants them physical advantages, such as increased strength, durability, and resilience. However, due to their size, they may lack agility and finesse in environments that are not designed to accommodate their stature. Their immense strength can inadvertently lead to accidental breakage of delicate objects like cups or chairs when they are not mindful of their surroundings.

# Language

The Helior have a distinctive language known as Heliorean, reflecting their deep connection to their ancestral roots. Heliorean is characterized by a deep and resonant tone, with words that carry a sense of power and authority. While Heliorean remains significant in combat orders and tribal rituals, the everyday language used by the Helior for common communication is Thaonese, influenced by their interactions with other races and cultures.

## Blood of the Helior Quality

The "Blood of the Helior" quality represents the inherent strength and resilience passed down from their giant ancestors. Helior characters gain a significant advantage in melee combat, inflicting an additional 2 points of damage with their melee attacks. Furthermore, due to their exceptional physical strength, they possess the ability to wield two-handed weapons effectively with a single hand, reducing the usual penalty associated with such weapons. However, the massive size of the Helior presents challenges when it comes to armor, marksmanship, and riding horses. All armor, marksmanship-related activities, and horseback riding require double the usual cost or carry a -3 penalty when used without additional training or adaptation.

# Inspiration Concept

The Helior draw inspiration from the ancient legends of giants, embodying their strength, power, and unwavering determination. Their society places a strong emphasis on physical prowess, martial training, courage, and loyalty. They are known for their strict codes of honor, discipline, and kinship, fostering a tightly-knit community focused on mutual protection and collective strength. The cultural aesthetics of the Helior blend elements of grandeur and ruggedness, reflecting their giant ancestry while embracing their human traits.

In the realm of role-playing, the Helior race carries echoes of the Spartans or Greek hoplites, embodying the ideals of disciplined warriors who excel in combat and are shaped by a strict and structured way of life. Their journey encompasses self-discovery, as they forge their own path while honoring the legacy of their ancestors.
