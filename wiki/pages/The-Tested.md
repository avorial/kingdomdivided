## [¶](#history) History

The Tested are one of the major foundations of the nation. Their formal name is the Society of the Letters, but they are only called that in the most formal of settings. Where the tested started is only known by them, most people feel they came over with the First Bellringer Crusade, but the fact that other Tested came with the Montimer gives credence to the theory that they come from the lands of The Lands of Fygor. Even as the Crusade was underway, they built houses of learning, bringing in the poor and the weak and giving them one year of substance while they prepared for their tests. Now there is an age limit that only people of 10 years old can come to the schools. After the Pact of Gold, children are not able to take their first test before their 12th birthday, so some children are able to get 2 years before their first test but after 12, you must take once test a year (until your 5th) to maintain the room and board of the Tested. While this is a paradise to the common folk, it is not luxurious, missing digits from frostbite is not uncommon in the ranks of elder tested. There is no prohibition in using your own money for better accommodations and some of the merchant classes who are studying for their tests live near the schools in local inns or boarded with family members.

Most cities of note have at least a small school for the tested and only those who have committed to learning or have a ring are allowed inside. The first ring every tested must pass is the ability to read and write in detail, people who already knew how to read and write commonly take their first ring in learning a foreign language. As knowledge already known, can not be worthy of a ring.

Secret legends among the Mikado are that the Tested are actually slaves to the Kadalan masters, that their rings show the value of a slave as with more rings it's harder to do manual work.

## [¶](#the-pact-of-gold) The Pact of Gold

In 34 RE, The Tested became more than just a society of the educated. They were elevated to being the cornerstone of the Imperial bureaucracy. Every 5th ring tested is given the a job by the government if they request it. From being court advisors, tax collectors, to even the Privy Council. The Pact of Gold has made the tested the face of the government as many of the tested have come from the common folk it has been a boon to the nation as a whole.

## [¶](#the-test) The Test

The Test happens only on the darkest night of any given month, with the axiom being “*Learning I can know, By being Tested I can understand*”. A person is stripped of all of their clothing and given a old hemp robe. The test is always written, and answers are judged by the most learned Tested in the city. Those that fail, disappear. A letter might be sent to their family, with a few personal possessions, but a body is never found. That is all that is known to an outsider. Every person who has earned their first ring also knows of the the oath.

## [¶](#the-oath) The Oath

Every tested is given a version of the oath based on the number of rings they have, when they get their 5th ring and become a full member of the tested the oath does not change as they rise in the ranks above that.

### [¶](#first-oath) First Oath

*“By my blood and by my eyes I swear to aid all who wish to be tested, for brining a stranger in to the light is the greatest gift.”*

### [¶](#second-oath) Second Oath

*“By my hands and my words I swear to give the knowledge I learn with wisdom, for knowing can be as dangerous as ignorance”*

### [¶](#third-oath) Third Oath

*“By my strength and my hearing, I swear to keep the unseen as it is, for only though deeper senses can one find the next knowledge"*

### [¶](#fourth-oath) Fourth Oath

*“By my legs, and my taste, I swear to never stop the journey. For knowledge is a sea that one who stops swimming will drown”*

### [¶](#fifth-oath) Fifth Oath

*“By my life, and the lives of my line, I swear fealty for the learned are my family. Never will I restrain a Learned from knowing, Never will I aid an ignorant who wishes harm on my siblings of the word. Woe to they who try to tempt me to break this oath, and woe to me if I succumb to such temptation.”*

At the taking of the fifth oath, the tested lose their ability to have children. Then they are taken to a major hall of the tested and shown the gateways to other worlds, fully understanding that their inability to have children is what powers the portals. ## [¶](#retirement) Retirement

Older tested are given the choice to leave this world, and be in a hall of the tested where they will not age as long as they still can learn, every “ring” they earn gives them another year of life. This “legend" is commonly said to people when they are asked about giving away their ability to have children. The statement in itself is a great example of the Tested saying an unbelievable truth to hide the deeper truths they have hidden.

## [¶](#payment-and-the-support-of-the-tested) Payment and the Support of the Tested

Almost all noble houses pay some taxes to the Tested directly. This is not an imperial mandate, but more it is better to keep an even playing field. It has been said that people who don't pay their Tested Tax, are not reprimanded but instead advice is given to their rivals until the House that is not paying changes their mind or is removed.  Also only houses that pay the tax are given Tested advisors.

## [¶](#politics-in-the-tested) Politics in the Tested

The hunger for wealth and the desire for power is not unknown by the Tested. Many feel because they can not make a family it makes a devil may care view in some of the Tested, take all you can. The vulgar term for people like this, are called those who "sold their rings". Others throw themselves in to the rulership of the Tested, workin on their 6 (and above rings). Those are the people who are given the greatest rewards such as rulership of the Tested in a domain called an “Ematha” or even to be able to politic for the privy council and even being named King of the Bells.

## [¶](#rules-mechanics) Rules & Mechanics

## [¶](#tested-quality) Tested Quality

You have undergone the 5 tests of becoming a full fledged member of the tested. This also means you can not have children AT ALL.

### [¶](#requires) Requires

Language 3, Cunning 3 & Knowledge Focus

### [¶](#perks) Perks

1. You add your ranks in Cunning to any Knowledge or Will Test. 1. If Lowborn: You are now linked to the house fortunes of where you are assigned or the Imperial House. (You must pick this for every promotion, and can not change it until you are promoted.) All glory you receive goes to the choice you made. 2. if Highborn: Can spend one more Destiny to have the freedom to give to your birth house, or the Imperial house. If you do not spend this destiny, you can only give to one. The issue is you can never rise in the ranks of the tested if you do not invest in the imperial house.
2. Your status is a 3. It can only be raised by the Tested (such as becoming a Ematha) or the Emperor himself (such as joining the privy council or king of the bells).
