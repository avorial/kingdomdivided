## [¶](#the-esten-rooted-in-change-a-culture-of-living-memory) The Esten: Rooted in Change A Culture of Living Memory

The Esten are often seen by outsiders as rigid, ancestral traditionalists—keepers of old laws, slow rituals, and oral epics passed down through generations. But within Esten lands, change flows like groundwater beneath still earth. They are a people of memory, not stagnation. Like the trees of their sacred groves, they grow imperceptibly, entwining new truths around old roots.

Their society is built on Tahl’mey—the binding of generational wisdom and lived experience. Every family bears ancestral lines not only in name but in practice.

Grandmother's hands, mother's songs, and uncle’s prayers are sacred texts. Even minor decisions are weighed against collective memory. The Esten’s magic is similarly woven through memory and presence.

Their oldest spiritual tradition, Angalaglassi, reveres the shadow realm—not as a place of death, but as a mirror of the living world where ancestral spirits reside, advise, and occasionally intervene.

## [¶](#the-angalaglassi-reborn) The Angalaglassi Reborn

With the intervention of Angalaglassi spirits during the Battle of Talmart—when a monstrous bellringer war-beast threatened to devour the region—the old ways found undeniable power in the modern world. A new generation of Esten youth, especially those born under rare moons or marked by vision-dreams, have begun hearing the “call of the shades.” These called ones undergo the Rite of Joining, a nightlong ritual in which they enter a death-trance and return changed—imbued with spectral sight, dream-walking ability, or deep ancestral memory. These “Angalaglassi Reborn” are viewed with both awe and suspicion. Many are now potent spellcasters whose power is drawn from ancestral pacts and memory-warping incantations. Some even bear visible signs of shadow-bonding: eyes blackened with star-light, whispers following their steps, or ancestral glyphs burning through their skin during storms. While deeply valued in Esten circles for their power and connection, they have sparked unease in the greater Empire—especially among Mikado inquisitors and Belllander nobles, who fear they represent a breach between the realms of the living and the dead.

## [¶](#the-esten-in-the-mikado-empire) The Esten in the Mikado Empire

Though once spurned by the Belllander elite, the Esten have carved out vital roles within the Mikado, thanks in large part to their two great houses:

### [¶](#house-thaphicare-the-earthblooded) House Thaphicare – The Earthblooded

As the ancestral cultivators of the Thaphin Plains, House Thaphicare is the beating heart of Esten endurance. Their lands feed the Empire. Their rituals sanctify the soil. Their wisdom, rooted in community and tradition, gives them authority without formal title. Cultural Notes: The Song of Names is sung during every harvest, a litany of ancestors dating back to the first Thaphin exile. They use chime-bells made of clay and bone to signal seasonal transitions and communal gatherings. Each child of the house is given a soil-blessing, planting a small seed in their skin that grows into a tiny vine, symbolizing rootedness.

### [¶](#house-lulumia-the-velvet-knives) House Lulumia – The Velvet Knives

Masters of whispers and silk diplomacy, House Lulumia occupies a different battlefield: that of the Imperial Court. Known for their unmatched knowledge of the Mikado legal system and the rituals of imperial bureaucracy, they act as covert protectors of Esten interests across the empire. Cultural Notes: Every member keeps a mask of silence—a carved porcelain half-mask—used during secret rituals or blackmail negotiations. The house maintains a ledger of ancestral bargains, detailing oaths owed and favors extracted from generations past. Rumors persist that they can “wake” the spirits of old enemies to whisper secrets in their ears.

New Faction: The Nightweavers' Assembly As the Angalaglassi Reborn grow in number, some have begun forming their own spiritual-political alliance: the Nightweavers' Assembly. Though not a formal house, they operate as a secretive cult-like organization within Esten society, focused on reclaiming ancient power and restoring the full practice of Angalaglassi rites across the Mikado. Some believe the Assembly seeks not just integration, but transformation—shaping the Empire into something new through spiritual convergence. Others fear they are building a theocracy beneath the surface.

## [¶](#esten-language-and-concepts) Esten Language and Concepts

- Tahl’mey – The sacred binding of memory, ancestry, and action.
- Glassian Rite – A spiritual calling to shadow and ancestral presence.
- Echo-Flesh – The subtle signs of possession or communion with the dead. Moon-Blooded – Children born under auspicious moons, more likely to be called by the Angalaglassi.
