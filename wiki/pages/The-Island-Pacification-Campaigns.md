## [¶](#the-island-pacification-campaigns-of-the-makado-empire-years-18221830-ah-after-the-heartbeat) The Island Pacification Campaigns of the Makado Empire Years 1822–1830 AH. (After the Heartbeat)

Supreme Commander: Lady Suria Abbyean, Flame-Admiral of the Red Banner Fleet Territories Annexed: Worsouk, Jakou, Kingfisher Isle, Yedini, Jade Isle, Mon-ra-ki, the Saffron Isles Climactic Event: The Shattering of Drelm’s Point

## [¶](#prelude-the-sea-between-the-belllands-and-the-empire) Prelude: The Sea Between the Belllands and the Empire

For generations, the fragmented island nations between the mainland of the Bellands and the Makado Archipelago played a dangerous game of neutrality. Rich in spices, dyes, and sacred minerals, these islands thrived by pitting the imperial titans against one another. But in the 17th Century After the Heartbeat, the Salamander Throne declared that no more buffer would be tolerated. The Worm Gods demanded a clean bridge of dominion to the northern continent. This declaration gave rise to the Sea-Smothering Years, and to one of the most revered and mourned commanders in the empire's long and brutal history: Lady Suria Abbyean.

## [¶](#the-flame-admiral-ascends) The Flame-Admiral Ascends

Lady Suria Abbyean, daughter of High General Tobari the First Flame, was born into imperial nobility with both privilege and burden. Though her name was noble, she made her own legend as a tactician of the Red Banner Fleet, the Empire’s fire-based naval armada. Trained in temple schools and on scorched deckwood, Suria rose through the ranks not by inheritance but by relentless cunning and a near-mythic sense for tide and timing. When the Ai Hoku selected her to lead the Northern Expansion, few doubted the outcome—only how many would be left to speak of it.

## [¶](#the-conquest-of-the-isles) The Conquest of the Isles

The campaign began with covert influence: Mikado agents ignited revolts, seeded promises of wealth, and arranged ritual marriages with island elite. Then came the Red Banners—burning sails on the horizon.

Each island fell in sequence:

1. Worsouk: Brought low by coin and compromise.
2. Jakou: Its obsidian towers shattered by ember bombardment.
3. Kingfisher Isle: Welcomed Makado banners, fearful of pirates.
4. Yedini: Its harbor burned from within by saboteurs loyal to Suria.
5. Jade Isle: Surrendered after Suria's ritual duel with their Sea Beast.
6. Mon-ra-ki: Its emerald jungles burned with a forest fire fed by naval bombardment.
7. The Saffron Isles:The final, bloodiest campaign—six years of resistance.

The people of the Empire began to call her “Sea-Mother of Ash”—a title both sacred and ominous.

## [¶](#the-shattering-of-drelms-point-1830-ah) The Shattering of Drelm’s Point (1830 AH)

The Bellringer Navy, long dominant in the southern waters, had been slow to respond to the speed of the Empire’s assault. But when they finally gathered their great fleet under Admiral Corven Rameriz at Drelm’s Point, they brought nearly all their remaining ships—over 300 sail. Lady Suria, against every voice of caution, ordered the Red Banner Fleet to meet them head-on. In the narrow straits known as Drelm’s Teeth, (Between Almirn and the Jade Isle) the sea itself became a forge. Fire ships, barbed mines, and salamander-blessed warbeasts turned the water red. At the height of the battle, Suria personally led a boarding assault against Admiral Rameriz's flagship. She succeeded. And she died—pierced through the ribs by a Bellringer blade, burned from the flame-beasts released in the fray. Her body, according to tradition, was wrapped in crimson sailcloth and offered to the sea amid chanting of the Worm Litany. Her final command was obeyed to the letter: “Drown the sun in ash. Let nothing sail without the Ai Hoku blessing.” Her death, though tragic, solidified the victory. The Bellringer navy was destroyed. The seas were now under the salamander banner.

## [¶](#legacy-the-red-path-to-the-belllands) Legacy: The Red Path to the Belllands

Though the pirates of Hirmfax Cay remain a persistent menace, and sea lanes are far from tame, the Makado Empire has never again faced a true naval rival. The Island Campaigns created a bridgehead for the eventual land invasion of the Belllands, and secured a generation of maritime dominance. Lady Suria Abbyean is now revered in naval academies, household shrines, and military cults as both a saint and a caution: “Even the sea must be fed.” Her image is often depicted with a flaming crown, one foot on a coiled serpent, the other planted on a shattered anchor—her sword raised toward the western sun she helped extinguish.
